# Events of March 28, 2014
======================================================

### News

* **Russia annexes Crimea**: In a move widely condemned by the international community, Russia formally annexes Ukraine's Crimean Peninsula, which had been under Russian control since February. [1]
* **Floods hit Malaysia**: Heavy rainfall and flash floods affect thousands of people in Malaysia, with over 10,000 homes damaged or destroyed. [2]

### Sports

* **Boston Marathon bombing suspect convicted**: Dzhokhar Tsarnaev is found guilty of using a weapon of mass destruction and killing four people in the 2013 Boston Marathon bombing. [3]
* **NBA Playoffs: Spurs defeat Mavericks**: The San Antonio Spurs defeat the Dallas Mavericks 93-89 in Game 6 to win their first-round playoff series, advancing to the Western Conference semifinals. [4]

### Culture

* **Pulitzer Prizes announced**: The Pulitzer Prize Board announces its winners for 2014, including the Pulitzer Prize for Fiction going to Donna Tartt's novel "The Goldfinch". [5]
* **Grammys: Daft Punk wins Album of the Year**: The French electronic duo Daft Punk wins the Grammy Award for Best Album with their album "Random Access Memories" at the 56th Annual Grammy Awards. [6]

### History

* **Soviet dissident Vladimir Bukovsky dies**: Vladimir Bukovsky, a prominent Soviet dissident and human rights activist, passes away at the age of 78. [7]
* **First commercial flight of Airbus A350**: The Airbus A350-900 makes its first commercial flight with launch customer Qatar Airways, marking a major milestone in aviation history. [8]

### Other

* **Ebola outbreak spreads in West Africa**: The Ebola virus disease outbreak continues to spread across Guinea, Liberia, and Sierra Leone, with over 25,000 reported cases and nearly 10,000 deaths by the end of March. [9]
* **Earthquake hits Chile**: A magnitude 6.7 earthquake strikes off the coast of Chile, causing some damage but no reported fatalities or injuries. [10]
