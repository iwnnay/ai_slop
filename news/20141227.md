# Events of December 27th, 2014

**News**

* **Syrian Civil War**: The Syrian government claimed to have retaken the strategic city of Adra from Islamist rebels, according to reports. [1]
* **Ebola Outbreak**: The World Health Organization (WHO) reported that Ebola had spread to Guinea's capital city, Conakry, bringing the total number of countries affected by the outbreak to seven. [2]

**Sports**

* **NBA**: The Cleveland Cavaliers traded center Andrew Bynum and a 2015 first-round draft pick to the Chicago Bulls in exchange for Luol Deng. [3]
* **MLS Cup**: New York Red Bulls won their third MLS Cup title with a 5-4 penalty shootout victory over Sporting Kansas City. [4]

**Cultural**

* **Film**: The animated film "The Interview" starring Seth Rogen and James Franco, which was initially banned by Sony Pictures due to threats from North Korean hackers, was released in limited theaters across the United States. [5]
* **Music**: American rapper Drake dropped his fourth studio album, "Nothing Was the Same", which debuted at number one on the US Billboard 200 chart. [6]

**Historical**

* **Soviet Union**: On this day in 1974, Soviet cosmonauts Vladislav Volkov and Nikolai Rukavishnikov landed safely back on Earth after spending 27 days in space aboard the Salyut 3 spacecraft. [7]
* **World War II**: December 27th marks the anniversary of the Battle of Nanking, a brutal massacre committed by Japanese soldiers against civilians and prisoners of war during World War II. [8]

**Other**

* **Weather**: A powerful winter storm hit the northeastern United States, causing widespread power outages and disrupting transportation networks. [9]
* **Space Exploration**: NASA's Orion spacecraft successfully completed its first parachute deployment test at the US Army Yuma Proving Ground in Arizona, a crucial step towards sending humans to Mars. [10]
