# Events of January 5th, 2014

## 1. The New Year's Eve storm in the United States

A powerful winter storm swept through the eastern United States, bringing heavy snowfall and high winds to many areas. The storm caused over 500,000 power outages and left thousands without electricity.

North Korea threatened to launch a "surgical strike" on key targets in South Korea if Seoul continued to conduct annual joint military exercises with the United States. The threats were seen as an attempt to deter Washington from conducting further military drills in the region.

The Indian government announced plans to build a new capital city, Amaravati, to replace the current capital, Hyderabad. The decision was aimed at reducing congestion and improving living conditions in the current capital.

A giant iceberg measuring approximately 11,000 square kilometers (4,250 sq mi) broke off from Antarctica's Larsen C ice shelf. The event raised concerns about climate change and its impact on global sea levels.

The European Union agreed to impose economic sanctions on Ukraine in response to the country's political crisis. The sanctions were aimed at targeting those responsible for the violence and unrest in Ukraine.

## 6. Russia-Ukraine gas dispute escalates

A dispute between Russia and Ukraine over natural gas prices escalated, with both sides imposing restrictions on gas supplies to each other. The conflict left many countries in Europe facing potential shortages of energy supplies.

## 7. A powerful earthquake strikes off the coast of Japan

A magnitude 6.8 earthquake struck off the coast of Japan, causing widespread panic and triggering tsunami warnings. Fortunately, there were no reports of major damage or loss of life from the quake.

## 8. The Israeli military announces plans to build a new wall in the West Bank

The Israeli military announced plans to build a new barrier along the West Bank border with Jordan, aimed at reducing smuggling and illegal immigration. The move was seen as an attempt to strengthen Israel's defenses against external threats.

## 9. A massive fire breaks out at a chemical plant in Belgium

A large fire broke out at a chemical plant in Belgium, releasing toxic chemicals into the air. The incident caused widespread concern about environmental and health risks from the smoke.

## 10. The French Open tennis tournament concludes in Paris

The 2014 French Open tennis tournament concluded in Paris, with Rafael Nadal winning his ninth French Open title by defeating Novak Djokovic in the men's singles final. Serena Williams won her third consecutive French Open women's singles title, beating Maria Sharapova in the final.
