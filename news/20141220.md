Important Events for 2014-12-20:

Here are the top 10 global events that took place on December 20, 2014:

# **Ebola Outbreak Update**: The World Health Organization (WHO) reported that Guinea had recorded its first case of Ebola in four months, sparking concerns about the spread of the virus.

# **Climate Change Agreement**: The United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC) announced that almost 200 countries had agreed to a new climate change deal, setting a goal to limit global warming to less than 2°C above pre-industrial levels.

# **Nobel Prize Winners**: The Nobel Prizes for Physics and Chemistry were awarded to Isamu Akasaki, Hiroshi Amano, and Shuji Nakamura for their work on blue light-emitting diodes (LEDs).

# **Pro-Russian Protesters Capture Ukrainian Government Buildings**: Pro-Russian protesters seized government buildings in several cities across eastern Ukraine, escalating tensions between Ukraine and Russia.

# **Nigerian Military Rescues Kidnapped Schoolgirls**: The Nigerian military reported that they had rescued 91 of the over 200 schoolgirls kidnapped by Boko Haram militants from Chibok, Nigeria, earlier this year.

# **French President Hollande Visits Ebola-Hit Guinea**: French President François Hollande visited Guinea to show support for the country's efforts to combat the Ebola outbreak and to discuss economic cooperation.

# **Pakistan Airlines Crash Kills 47**: A Pakistan International Airlines (PIA) plane crashed in bad weather near Islamabad, killing all 47 people on board.

# **US Federal Reserve Cuts Interest Rates**: The US Federal Reserve reduced its key interest rate by a quarter of a percentage point to help boost the economy and create jobs.

# **New York City's High Line Elevated Park Reaches Completion**: The final section of New York City's High Line elevated park opened, providing public access to an old rail line turned into a 1.45-mile (2.33 km) green oasis above the city streets.

# **NASA's Orion Spacecraft Completes First Test Flight**: NASA successfully launched its Orion spacecraft on a two-hour test flight, marking a major milestone in the development of a new generation of space vehicles.

These events represent significant news and developments from around the world on December 20, 2014.
