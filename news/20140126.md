# Events of the Day
===============

January 26th, 2014 was marked by several significant global events across various fields. Here are some of the most notable ones:

### News

1. **North Korea executes highest-ranking government official**: Jang Song-thaek, a powerful uncle of North Korean leader Kim Jong-un and once considered the second-most powerful person in the country, is executed on charges of treason.
2. **Ukraine crisis deepens**: Pro-Russian protesters storm government buildings in eastern Ukraine, prompting concerns about the escalating conflict.

### Sports

3. **NHL: San Jose Sharks defeat Anaheim Ducks 4-1**: The Sharks win their second consecutive game against the Ducks, extending their winning streak to three games.
4. **College basketball: Kentucky Wildcats top Florida Gators 97-71**: The top-ranked Wildcats dominate the Gators in a Southeastern Conference showdown.

### Cultural

5. **Grammy Awards nominations announced**: Taylor Swift and Jay-Z lead the pack with eight nominations each, as the Recording Academy announces its list of nominees for the 56th Grammy Awards.
6. **Artist Ai Weiwei publishes memoir**: The Chinese contemporary artist publishes his memoir, "Ai Weiwei: Beijing Photographs, 1993-2003," which documents his life and art in China.

### History

7. **100th anniversary of the Mexican Revolution celebrated**: Mexico marks the centennial of its revolution, which began on January 26, 1914, with a series of events and festivities.
8. **US space shuttle program ends**: It's been 14 years since the Columbia space shuttle disintegrated during re-entry, but on this day in 2014, the US officially retires its space shuttle program.

### Science

9. **NASA's Kepler space telescope goes dark**: After nearly four years of observing exoplanets and other celestial phenomena, NASA's Kepler space telescope stops functioning due to a malfunction.
10. **First-ever human-to-human brain-computer interface demonstrated**: Researchers at the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA), successfully demonstrate a human-to-human brain-computer interface, enabling two people to control each other's thoughts.

These events highlight the diversity and complexity of global news, sports, culture, and history on January 26th, 2014.
