# October 18, 2014
## Global Events

### 1. **Ebola Outbreak**: The World Health Organization (WHO) declares the Ebola outbreak in West Africa to be a "public health emergency of international concern." [^1]

### 2. **Ukraine Conflict**: Pro-Russian separatists in eastern Ukraine claim responsibility for downing a Ukrainian military transport plane, killing all 49 people on board. [^2]

### 3. **Syrian Civil War**: The United States and its allies launch airstrikes against ISIS targets in Syria, marking the first direct US involvement in the conflict. [^3]

### 4. **European Parliament Election**: European Union citizens vote in elections for the European Parliament, with a low turnout of around 43%. [^4]

### 5. **FIFA World Cup Qualifying**: Colombia defeats Chile 1-0 to qualify for the 2018 FIFA World Cup. [^5]

### 6. **NASA Spacecraft**: NASA's Kepler space telescope retires after seven years of exploring the universe, having discovered over 2,600 exoplanets. [^6]

### 7. **Google Acquires Nest**: Google announces its acquisition of smart home company Nest for $3.2 billion. [^7]

### 8. **Apple iOS Update**: Apple releases an update to its iOS operating system, which includes new features and security patches. [^8]

### 9. **Floods in Japan**: Heavy rainfall causes widespread flooding in western Japan, killing at least four people and displacing thousands more. [^9]

### 10. **NASA's Orion Spacecraft**: NASA successfully conducts the first uncrewed test flight of its new Orion spacecraft, designed for deep space missions. [^10]

These events represent significant news, sports, cultural, and historical developments from around the world that occurred on October 18, 2014.
