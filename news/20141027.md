**Global Events**

October 27, 2014
----------------

### 1. Ebola Outbreak: Liberia Reports Highest Daily Death Toll

* The World Health Organization (WHO) reported that Liberia had seen its highest daily death toll from the Ebola virus outbreak, with 69 new cases and 27 deaths in the past 24 hours.

### 2. Hong Kong Protests: Occupy Central Movement Enters Third Week

* The pro-democracy protest movement, also known as the Umbrella Revolution, entered its third week, with tens of thousands of people still occupying key intersections in Hong Kong's financial district.

### 3. Russian Airstrikes: Ukraine Accuses Russia of Bombing Its Territory

* Ukrainian authorities accused Russian forces of conducting airstrikes on Ukrainian territory, causing widespread damage and civilian casualties.

### 4. European Parliament Elections: Turnout Hits Record Low

* The European Parliament elections saw a record-low turnout of just 42.2%, with many voters staying away from the polls in protest at the lack of transparency and accountability among politicians.

### 5. Nobel Prize in Physics: Isamu Akasaki, Hiroshi Amano Win for LED Development

* Japanese scientists Isamu Akasaki and Hiroshi Amano were awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics for their work on developing blue LEDs, which has led to the creation of white LEDs and improved energy efficiency.

### 6. FIFA World Cup Qualifiers: Brazil Beats Argentina to Reach Final Round

* Brazil defeated Argentina 2-1 at home to secure a spot in the final round of South American qualifying for the 2018 FIFA World Cup.

### 7. US Presidential Election: Hillary Clinton Formally Announces Candidacy

* Former Secretary of State Hillary Clinton formally announced her candidacy for the Democratic presidential nomination, marking the beginning of her campaign against Republican rival Donald Trump.

### 8. European Parliament Passes Data Protection Regulation

* The European Parliament passed a data protection regulation aimed at strengthening online privacy and security for EU citizens, with the aim of giving individuals more control over their personal data.

### 9. NASA's Orion Spacecraft Completes First Flight Test

* NASA successfully completed its first flight test of the Orion spacecraft, which is designed to take humans to Mars in the 2030s.

### 10. Nobel Prize in Chemistry: Eric Betzig and Stefan W. Hell Win for Super-Resolution Microscopy

* American chemist Eric Betzig and German biophysicist Stefan W. Hell were awarded the Nobel Prize in Chemistry for developing super-resolution microscopy techniques that have enabled scientists to visualize biological samples at the molecular level.
