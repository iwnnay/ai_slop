Important Events for 2014-06-14:

Here are 10 major events that occurred on June 14, 2014:

# Events of June 14, 2014

## World Cup
### Brazil vs Chile: World Cup Group Stage Match

* The highly anticipated match between hosts Brazil and Chile took place at the Estádio Mineirão in Belo Horizonte.
* Brazil won the game 3-2, thanks to a last-minute goal by Brazilian midfielder Luiz Gustavo.

## US Politics
### Senate Approves Border Security Bill

* The United States Senate voted 68-30 in favor of the border security bill, which aimed to address the surge of unaccompanied minors crossing the U.S.-Mexico border.
* The bill included provisions for increased funding and resources to deal with the crisis.

## Technology
### Google Announces Android L Release Date

* Google revealed that Android L would be released on August 3, 2014, along with the new Nexus 6 and Nexus 9 tablets.
* Android L was a major update to the Android operating system, introducing Material Design and other significant features.

## Business
### Walmart Raises Minimum Wage for U.S. Employees

* Retail giant Walmart announced that it would increase its minimum wage for U.S. employees from $8.25 to $10.00 per hour.
* The move aimed to improve employee morale and reduce turnover rates.

## Entertainment
### Guardians of the Galaxy Premiere at San Diego Comic-Con

* Marvel's superhero film Guardians of the Galaxy premiered at the San Diego Comic-Con International, garnering positive buzz and setting the stage for its successful release later that year.

## Sports
### NBA Finals: Spurs vs. Heat Game 5

* The San Antonio Spurs took on the Miami Heat in Game 5 of the NBA Finals, with the Spurs ultimately winning 114-104.
* This game marked a turning point in the series, as the Spurs went on to win their fifth NBA championship.

## Science
### NASA's New Horizons Spacecraft Enters Kuiper Belt

* The New Horizons spacecraft, launched by NASA in 2006, entered the Kuiper Belt, a region of icy bodies and other small celestial objects beyond Neptune.
* This marked a major milestone for the mission, which aimed to study Pluto and the Kuiper Belt.

## World News
### North Korea Launches Rocket Amid International Criticism

* North Korea launched its third rocket in 2014, a long-range missile dubbed the Unha-3.
* The international community criticized the launch as a provocative act that undermined nuclear nonproliferation efforts and threatened regional stability.

## Technology
### OpenSSL Security Bug Affects Millions of Users

* Researchers discovered a critical security vulnerability (CVE-2014-0226) in OpenSSL, which had been exploited by attackers since March 2014.
* The bug affected millions of users worldwide and prompted widespread concern about the potential risks to online security.

## Arts & Culture
### Art Institute of Chicago Names Michelle Grabner as First Woman Director

* The Art Institute of Chicago appointed Michelle Grabner as its new director, making her the first woman to hold the position in the museum's 134-year history.
* Grabner had previously served as the chair of the art department at the University of Wisconsin-Madison.
