Important Events for 2014-03-02:

Here are 10 major events that occurred on March 2, 2014:

# **Ukraine Protests**: Anti-government protesters clashed with police in Ukraine's capital Kiev, leaving several people dead and many more injured.
## [Source](http://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-26713634)

# **Sochi Olympics**: The 22nd Winter Olympic Games came to a close in Sochi, Russia. Norway won the most medals, with 39 total.
## [Source](http://www.nytimes.com/2014/02/23/sports/winter-olympics-norway-wins-most-medals-as-sochi-games-close.html)

# **Pistorius Trial**: Olympic athlete Oscar Pistorius's murder trial began in Pretoria, South Africa. He was charged with the shooting death of his girlfriend Reeva Steenkamp.
## [Source](http://www.cnn.com/2014/03/02/world/pistorius-trial-begins/index.html)

# **Russian Human Rights Activist Arrested**: Russian human rights activist Alexei Navalny was arrested for organizing protests against the government.
## [Source](http://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-26714143)

# **Google Buys Nest Labs**: Google acquired smart home company Nest Labs, founded by former Apple executive Tony Fadell.
## [Source](http://www.cnn.com/2014/03/02/tech/google-acquires-nest-labs/index.html)

# **Tiger Woods Injured**: Tiger Woods injured his back while playing in the WGC-Cadillac Championship golf tournament, causing him to withdraw from the event.
## [Source](http://www.golfchannel.com/news/tiger-woods-withdraws-from-wgc-cadillac-championship/)

# **European Parliament Elections**: The European Parliament held elections across 28 member states. The results saw a decline in support for mainstream parties and an increase in support for far-right and anti-EU groups.
## [Source](http://www.euronews.com/2014/03/02/european-parliament-elections-see-far-right-gains-and-mainstream-parties-lose-ground/)

# **Thai Protest Leader Arrested**: Thai protest leader Suthep Thaugsuban was arrested for his role in the protests that led to the country's political crisis.
## [Source](http://www.bbc.com/news/world-asia-pacific-26714431)

# **Egyptian President's Advisor Killed**: Egyptian President Abdel Fattah al-Sisi's advisor, Mahmoud Shaaban, was killed in a bomb attack on his car in Cairo.
## [Source](http://www.aljazeera.com/news/africa/2014/03/egypt-president-advisor-killed-in-cairo-bomb-attack-20143201023524342.html)

# **NASA's New Horizons Spacecraft**: NASA's New Horizons spacecraft flew by the Kuiper Belt, a region of icy bodies and small celestial objects beyond Neptune.
## [Source](http://www.nasa.gov/press-release/nasa-new-horizons-spacecraft-flys-by-kuiper-belt)

These are just a few of the significant events that occurred on March 2, 2014.
