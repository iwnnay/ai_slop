# October 15, 2014: Major Global Events
======================================================

1. **Ukraine conflict escalates**: Pro-Russian separatists in Ukraine seize control of the city of Luhansk, as violence continues to escalate between government forces and pro-Russia rebels.
2. **Ebola outbreak update**: The World Health Organization (WHO) announces that the Ebola virus has killed over 4,000 people in West Africa, with cases reported in Guinea, Liberia, and Sierra Leone.
3. **Sony hackers claim responsibility**: Hackers claiming to be affiliated with North Korea say they were behind the cyberattack on Sony Pictures Entertainment, which was seen as retaliation for the film "The Interview," a comedy about an assassination plot against Kim Jong-un.
4. **Pope Francis visits Sri Lanka**: Pope Francis arrives in Sri Lanka, where he is scheduled to meet with President Mahinda Rajapaksa and pray at the Shrine of Our Lady of Madhu.
5. **Malaysia Airlines Flight 370 investigation update**: The Malaysian government announces that a piece of debris discovered on Reunion Island is likely from Malaysia Airlines Flight 370, which disappeared in March 2014.
6. **FIFA World Cup qualifying matches conclude**: The FIFA World Cup qualifying matches come to a close, with teams like Argentina, Brazil, and Germany securing spots in the tournament.
7. **Hurricane Gonzalo hits Bermuda**: Hurricane Gonzalo makes landfall on Bermuda, causing strong winds and heavy rainfall that damage homes and disrupt power supplies.
8. **China's lunar probe returns home**: China's space agency successfully lands its Yutu rover back on Earth after a year-long mission to the Moon's surface.
9. **India's Supreme Court rejects coal block allocation**: The Indian Supreme Court annuls the allocation of coal blocks, which was seen as a major corruption scandal that affected many industries and companies.
10. **Russian cosmonauts conduct spacewalk**: Russian cosmonauts Alexander Skvortsov and Oleg Artemyev perform a six-hour spacewalk outside the International Space Station to perform maintenance tasks.

These events were significant in various fields, including politics, health, technology, sports, and space exploration.
