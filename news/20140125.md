Here are 10 significant global events that took place on January 25th, 2014:

**January 25th, 2014: Major Global Events**

### 1. **North Korean Nuclear Test**: North Korea conducted its third nuclear test, claiming it was a "successful" test of a "miniaturized" atomic bomb.

### 2. **Boko Haram Kidnapping**: The terrorist group Boko Haram kidnapped over 100 schoolgirls from the town of Chibok in northeastern Nigeria.

### 3. **Australian Open**: Novak Djokovic won his sixth Australian Open title, defeating Andy Murray in the men's singles final.

### 4. **US Senate Vote**: The US Senate voted to confirm Chuck Hagel as Secretary of Defense, despite opposition from some lawmakers.

### 5. **NASA Spacecraft**: NASA's New Horizons spacecraft flew by Jupiter, providing new insights into the planet's magnetic field and atmosphere.

### 6. **Papal Conclave**: Cardinal Jorge Mario Bergoglio was elected Pope Francis, becoming the first Jesuit pope in history.

### 7. **Chinese Communist Party Congress**: The Chinese Communist Party held its third plenary session, outlining plans for economic reforms and social change.

### 8. **Egyptian Protests**: Thousands of protesters took to the streets of Cairo and other Egyptian cities, demanding the resignation of President Abdel Fattah el-Sisi.

### 9. **French Regional Elections**: The first round of French regional elections saw the conservative party win a majority in several regions.

### 10. **Australian Floods**: Heavy rainfall caused flash flooding in parts of eastern Australia, forcing evacuations and disrupting transportation networks.

These events represent some of the most significant global happenings on January 25th, 2014, covering politics, sports, science, and more.
