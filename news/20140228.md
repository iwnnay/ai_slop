Here are 10 major global events that occurred on February 28, 2014:

# Most Important Global Events of February 28, 2014
======================================================

## Crimea Votes to Leave Ukraine

On February 28, 2014, the Crimean parliament voted overwhelmingly in favor of holding a referendum to leave Ukraine and join Russia. This move was seen as a response to the ousting of Ukrainian President Viktor Yanukovych earlier that month.

## Russia Warns Against Foreign Intervention

Russian President Vladimir Putin warned against foreign intervention in Ukraine, saying it would be a "grave mistake" to try to influence the country's internal affairs.

## US and EU Impose Sanctions on Russia

In response to Russia's actions in Ukraine, the United States and European Union imposed economic sanctions on Russian officials and entities. This included freezing assets and imposing travel bans on key individuals.

## Winter Storm Hits Eastern US

A powerful winter storm brought significant snowfall and high winds to the eastern United States, causing thousands of flight cancellations and widespread power outages.

## NBA Trade Deadline Passes

The National Basketball Association's trade deadline passed on February 28, with several notable players changing teams as part of deals.

## Grammy Awards Held in Los Angeles

The 56th Annual Grammy Awards took place at the Staples Center in Los Angeles, California. Daft Punk, Macklemore & Ryan Lewis, and Lorde were among the big winners.

## Ukraine's Foreign Minister Resigns

Ukrainian Foreign Minister Andriy Deshchytsia resigned on February 28, citing a need to "contribute to the development of international relations" in a new role.

## Russia's President Putin Visits Crimean Peninsula

Russian President Vladimir Putin visited the Crimean Peninsula, where he met with local leaders and vowed to defend the region from what he called "foreign intervention."

## European Parliament Condemns Russia's Actions

The European Parliament passed a resolution condemning Russia's actions in Ukraine as a "violation of international law" and calling for an immediate withdrawal of Russian forces.

## Israel-Gaza Border Crossing Reopened

After a brief closure, the Israeli-Gaza border crossing was reopened on February 28, allowing Palestinians to travel to Israel for business or medical treatment.
