Important Events for 2014-01-24:

Here are 10 major global events that took place on January 24th, 2014:

# 1: **US and EU impose sanctions on Ukraine**

North Korea launched a long-range rocket, claiming it was part of a peaceful satellite launch mission. The move sparked international condemnation and concerns about North Korea's nuclear program.

Pope Francis created 19 new cardinals from around the world, marking the first consistory of his papacy. This event marked a significant shift in the Church's leadership and representation.

The Australian Open, one of the four Grand Slam tennis tournaments, began on January 24th, featuring top players like Novak Djokovic and Serena Williams.

Egyptian President Abdel Fattah el-Sisi met with Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu in Cairo to discuss regional security issues and peace talks.

The United Nations General Assembly held a special session to address the global threat of climate change, with leaders like Barack Obama and Angela Merkel calling for urgent action.

Chinese President Xi Jinping visited Vietnam, marking the first visit by a Chinese head of state in nearly a decade. The trip aimed to strengthen bilateral ties and resolve maritime disputes.

# 8: **Philippine government reports on typhoon recovery**

The Philippine government released its report on the recovery efforts following Typhoon Haiyan, which devastated parts of the country in November 2013.

# 9: **US Supreme Court hears same-sex marriage case**

The US Supreme Court heard arguments in the landmark Obergefell v. Hodges case, challenging state bans on same-sex marriage and paving the way for a major ruling on gay rights.

# 10: **NASA's Parker Solar Probe begins construction**

NASA announced the start of construction on its Parker Solar Probe mission, aiming to explore the Sun's corona and study solar wind phenomena.
