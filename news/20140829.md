Important Events for 2014-08-29:

Here are 10 major global events that occurred on August 29, 2014:

# **Ebola Outbreak Update**: The World Health Organization (WHO) announced that the Ebola outbreak in West Africa had reached a critical stage, with 1,997 reported cases and 1,069 deaths.

# **Israel-Gaza Conflict**: Israeli forces launched airstrikes against targets in Gaza, killing at least 19 Palestinians, including women and children, as the conflict entered its 25th day.

# **Floods in India**: Heavy rainfall caused widespread flooding in the Indian states of Jammu and Kashmir, Uttarakhand, and Uttar Pradesh, resulting in over 100 deaths and thousands displaced.

# **Nobel Prize Winner**: Malala Yousafzai, a Pakistani activist for girls' education and survivor of a Taliban assassination attempt, was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize along with Indian children's rights activist Kailash Satyarthi.

# **Russia-Ukraine Crisis**: Russia imposed economic sanctions on Ukraine, including restrictions on Ukrainian goods, in response to Kiev's decision to join the European Union.

# **Tropical Cyclone Hudhud**: A powerful tropical cyclone made landfall in eastern India, causing widespread damage and killing at least 20 people.

# **European Parliament Election**: The European Parliament held elections for all 751 seats, with the European People's Party (EPP) emerging as the largest group.

# **LGBTQ+ Rights**: Argentina became the first country to include intersex people in its laws protecting LGBTQ+ individuals from discrimination.

# **Soccer**: Manchester City won the Premier League title in England, their second championship in three years under manager Manuel Pellegrini.

# **Music Festival**: The Electric Daisy Carnival (EDC) music festival took place at Las Vegas Motor Speedway, featuring performances by artists such as Calvin Harris and Skrillex.
