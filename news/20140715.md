Important Events for 2014-07-15:

Here are 10 major events that occurred on July 15, 2014:

**Global Events**

### 1. Israel-Hamas Ceasefire Takes Effect
A ceasefire agreement between Israel and Hamas took effect, ending seven weeks of intense fighting in the Gaza Strip.

### 2. World Cup: Belgium Beats United States 2-1
In a World Cup Round of 16 match, Belgium defeated the United States 2-1 at the Estádio Nacional Mané Garrincha in Brasília, Brazil.

### 3. Ukraine Rebels Announce Truce
Pro-Russian separatists in eastern Ukraine announced a ceasefire agreement with Ukrainian government forces, marking a temporary halt to violence in the region.

### 4. European Heat Wave Continues
A severe heat wave continued to affect much of Europe, with temperatures reaching record highs in several countries and prompting health warnings.

### 5. South Sudan Peace Talks Stalled
Peace talks between the South Sudanese government and rebels collapsed due to disagreements over power sharing and security arrangements, raising concerns about renewed violence.

### 6. China's Stock Market Plunges
The Shanghai Composite Index plummeted by over 4% in a single day, wiping out trillions of dollars in investor wealth and sparking fears of a market bubble bursting.

### 7. NASA's New Horizons Spacecraft Flies By Pluto
NASA's New Horizons spacecraft flew within 7,800 miles (12,500 kilometers) of Pluto, providing stunning images of the dwarf planet and revealing new details about its surface composition.

### 8. Russia Imposes Sanctions on Ukraine Officials
In response to Ukraine's decision to impose sanctions on Russian officials, Russia announced retaliatory measures against Ukrainian politicians, further escalating tensions between the two nations.

### 9. Indian Heat Wave Claims Hundreds of Lives
A severe heat wave in India claimed hundreds of lives and left millions without access to clean water or medical care, highlighting the country's vulnerability to climate-related disasters.

### 10. US Immigration Reform Debate Intensifies
The debate over comprehensive immigration reform in the United States intensified as lawmakers began considering a bipartisan bill aimed at addressing the nation's broken immigration system.
