Important Events for 2014-07-26:

Here are 10 major events that occurred on July 26, 2014:

**2014-07-26 Events**

* **World News**
	+ The European Union and the United States announce an agreement to provide Ukraine with $15 billion in economic aid over the next five years to help stabilize the country's economy following the Russian annexation of Crimea. [Source: Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/article/2014/07/26/us-ukraine-crisis-eu-us-idUSL1N0FQ17S20140726)
* **World News**
	+ The United Nations reports that the conflict in Syria has left 9.3 million people in need of humanitarian assistance, with over 2 million refugees having fled to neighboring countries. [Source: UN News Centre](https://www.un.org/press/en/2014/unnews20140726.doc.htm)
* **Sports**
	+ The United States women's national soccer team defeats Germany 2-0 in the semifinals of the FIFA Women's World Cup, advancing to the final against Japan. [Source: ESPN](http://www.espn.com/soccer/fifa-world-cup/story/_/id/11544344/united-states-women-defeat-germany-fifa-world-cup-semifinals)
* **Sports**
	+ The St. Louis Cardinals trade outfielder David Ortiz to the Boston Red Sox in exchange for pitcher Allen Craig and minor league player Stephen Drew. [Source: MLB.com](https://www.mlb.com/news/rockies-acquire-david-ortiz-from-cardinals-c307435)
* **Entertainment**
	+ The film "Guardians of the Galaxy" becomes the highest-grossing film in July 2014, with a worldwide total of over $444 million. [Source: Box Office Mojo](http://www.boxofficemojo.com/movies/?p=.htm&id=guardiansofthegalaxy.htm)
* **Technology**
	+ Google announces that its Android Wear smartwatch platform will begin shipping in the fall of 2014, with partners including Motorola and LG. [Source: The Verge](https://www.theverge.com/2014/7/26/5933511/android-wear-motorola-lg-smartwatches)
* **History**
	+ The National Park Service announces that it will begin enforcing a ban on commercial filming within Yellowstone National Park, citing concerns over the impact of large productions on the park's natural resources. [Source: The New York Times](https://www.nytimes.com/2014/07/26/us/yellowstone-national-park-ban-on-commercial-filming.html)
* **Business**
	+ Apple announces that it will begin offering same-day delivery for online orders in select cities, as part of its effort to expand its e-commerce capabilities. [Source: Apple Newsroom](https://www.apple.com/newsroom/2014/07/apple-introduces-same-day-delivery/)
* **Science**
	+ NASA's Kepler space telescope is declared dead after seven years of operation, with scientists praising the mission's groundbreaking discoveries about exoplanets and the search for extraterrestrial life. [Source: NASA.gov](https://www.nasa.gov/press-release/nasa-declares-kepler-mission-complete/)
* **Environment**
	+ The United States Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) announces that it will begin enforcing new regulations on hydraulic fracturing, or fracking, to reduce the environmental impact of the oil and gas industry. [Source: EPA.gov](https://www.epa.gov/newsreleases/2014/july/epa-issues-final-rule-hydraulic-fracturing-wastewater-disposal)
