# Events on 2014-01-08
===============

1. **South Sudan Conflict Escalates**
Violence in South Sudan's capital Juba intensified, with reports of heavy gunfire and explosions. The conflict had already killed hundreds and displaced thousands.
2. **NASA Announces New Mars Rover Landing Site**
NASA announced that its new Mars rover, the Curiosity Rover, would land on a region of Mars known as Gale Crater on August 21st. This would be a major milestone in the search for life beyond Earth.
3. **Syrian Rebel Leader Killed**
Abu Bakr al-Baghdadi, the leader of the Islamic State of Iraq and Syria (ISIS), was killed in a US drone strike in eastern Syria. This marked a significant blow to ISIS's leadership.
4. **Nobel Prize Winners Announced**
The Nobel Prizes for 2013 were announced, with winners including Dr. Frans van der Meer (Physics), Dr. James E. Rothman and Dr. Randy W. Schekman (Physiology or Medicine), and Dr. Koichi Tanaka (Chemistry).
5. **European Central Bank Announces Stimulus Package**
The European Central Bank announced a stimulus package to boost the struggling Eurozone economy, including cutting interest rates and implementing quantitative easing.
6. **Mitt Romney Concedes in New Hampshire Primary**
In a major upset, Mitt Romney conceded defeat in the New Hampshire primary, marking a significant setback for his presidential bid.
7. **India Lifts Ban on US Chicken Imports**
After a 15-year ban, India lifted its restrictions on imports of US chicken, paving the way for increased trade between the two countries.
8. **Pope Francis Meets with Muslim Leaders**
In a historic meeting, Pope Francis met with Islamic leaders at the Vatican, marking a significant step towards interfaith dialogue and understanding.
9. **FIFA Announces 2014 World Cup Schedule**
The schedule for the 2014 FIFA World Cup was announced, featuring 64 matches played in 12 Brazilian cities from June 12th to July 13th.
10. **Golden Globes Awards Held**
The 71st Golden Globe Awards were held at the Beverly Hilton Hotel in Los Angeles, with winners including "American Hustle," "Breaking Bad," and Leonardo DiCaprio for Best Actor in a Drama.
