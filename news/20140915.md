# Monday, September 15, 2014
===============

1. **Ebola Outbreak Worsens**: The World Health Organization (WHO) reported that Ebola had spread to four countries in West Africa: Guinea, Liberia, Sierra Leone, and Nigeria. The outbreak had already claimed over 2,000 lives.
2. **Russian Invasion of Ukraine**: Ukrainian authorities accused Russia of sending troops into eastern Ukraine, sparking international concern over the escalating crisis.
3. **Ferguson Unrest Continues**: Protests continued in Ferguson, Missouri, following the shooting death of Michael Brown by police officer Darren Wilson. The incident sparked widespread civil unrest and calls for racial justice.
4. **NATO Summit Concludes**: The North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO) concluded its 2014 summit in Wales, with leaders agreeing to increase military spending and enhance cooperation against terrorism.
5. **Gaza Ceasefire Ends**: A ceasefire between Israel and Hamas ended after a brief respite from violence, as rockets continued to rain down on Israeli cities.
6. **US Federal Budget Passes**: The US Congress passed a $1.01 trillion federal budget for 2015, ending a months-long stalemate over spending priorities.
7. **NFL Player Suffers Concussion**: Washington Redskins quarterback Robert Griffin III left a game against the Philadelphia Eagles after suffering a concussion, raising concerns about player safety in professional football.
8. **Apple Acquires Beats Electronics**: Technology giant Apple acquired audio equipment manufacturer Beats Electronics for $3 billion, marking one of its largest-ever acquisitions.
9. **UN General Assembly Convenes**: The United Nations General Assembly opened its 69th session, with leaders from around the world gathering to discuss global issues like climate change and human rights.
10. **Soviet Submarine Discovered Off Coast**: A team of explorers discovered a Soviet submarine off the coast of Norway, believed to be one of several lost vessels that sank during World War II.
