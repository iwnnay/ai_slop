Important Events for 2014-10-25:

Here are 10 major global events that occurred on October 25, 2014:

# 2014 Ebola Outbreak
The World Health Organization (WHO) declared the Ebola outbreak in West Africa to be a Public Health Emergency of International Concern (PHEIC), sparking widespread concern and response efforts.

# Russian Airstrikes in Ukraine
Russia launched airstrikes against Ukrainian military positions in Donetsk, Donetsk People's Republic, marking a significant escalation of the conflict between Russia and Ukraine.

# Hong Kong Protests
Pro-democracy protesters occupied major streets in Hong Kong to protest the government's plans for electoral reforms, which they deemed inadequate. The protests continued until December 2014.

# US Senate Approves Military Authorization
The United States Senate passed the National Defense Authorization Act (NDAA) for Fiscal Year 2015, authorizing $577 billion for military spending and containing provisions related to terrorism and national security.

# Ebola Outbreak in Nigeria
Nigeria confirmed a case of Ebola, becoming the first country outside West Africa to report an outbreak of the disease. The incident raised concerns about the virus's potential spread beyond the affected regions.

# UK Government Survives No-Confidence Vote
The British government survived a no-confidence vote in Parliament, defeating a motion of no confidence brought by Labour Party leader Ed Miliband.

# Ukraine Ceasefire Agreement
Ukraine and Russian-backed separatists signed a ceasefire agreement to halt hostilities in eastern Ukraine. However, the truce was repeatedly violated, leading to further violence and conflict.

# US Stock Market Suffers Worst Loss Since 2011
The Dow Jones Industrial Average plummeted by over 300 points (2.4%), its worst loss since May 2012, as concerns about Ebola and global economic growth weighed on markets.

# Nobel Prize in Physics Awarded
The Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics to Isamu Akasaki, Hiroshi Amano, and Shuji Nakamura for their invention of efficient blue light-emitting diodes (LEDs).

# European Union Reaches Gas Deal with Ukraine
The European Union and Ukraine agreed on a gas supply deal, ensuring Ukraine's energy security and reducing its dependence on Russian natural gas imports.
