Important Events for 2014-02-20:

Here are 10 major global events that occurred on February 20, 2014:

**February 20, 2014**
====================

### News and Politics

1. **Syrian Peace Talks Begin**: The Syrian government and opposition groups began negotiating in Geneva to end the devastating civil war in Syria. [Source: BBC](http://www.bbc.com/news/world-middle-east-26243423)
2. **Russian Anti-Gay Law Upheld**: Russia's Supreme Court upheld a law banning "gay propaganda" among minors, sparking international criticism and concern over human rights. [Source: CNN](http://edition.cnn.com/2014/02/20/world/russia-supreme-court-upholds-anti-gay-law/)
3. **Thai Military Takes Control**: Thailand's military seized control of the government, following weeks of political unrest and a coup that ousted Prime Minister Yingluck Shinawatra. [Source: Al Jazeera](http://www.aljazeera.com/news/asia/2014/02/thailand-military-takes-control-20142151223411144.html)

### Sports

1. **Sochi Winter Olympics Close**: The 22nd Winter Olympic Games in Sochi, Russia, came to a close with the closing ceremony featuring performances by Russian musicians and athletes. [Source: NBC](https://www.nbcnews.com/storyline/sochi-olympics/winter-olympics-close-sochi-ceremony-nations-unite-n37491)

### Culture

1. **Polaroid Film Revived**: Polaroid announced it would revive its iconic instant film, allowing fans to purchase and use the nostalgic cameras once more. [Source: The Verge](https://www.theverge.com/2014/2/20/5442346/polaroid-film-revival-nostalgia)
2. **Google Glass Launches**: Google released its highly anticipated smart glasses, Google Glass, to developers and a select few, marking the beginning of a new era in wearable technology. [Source: TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2014/02/20/google-glass-is-now-available-to-developers-and-a-select-few/)

### History

1. **NASA's Kepler Spacecraft Runs Out of Fuel**: NASA announced that its Kepler space telescope had run out of fuel, ending its nine-year mission to detect exoplanets. [Source: NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/press-release/nasa-s-kepler-space-telescope-run-out-of-fuel)
2. **Vatican Confirms Pope Francis' Encyclical**: The Vatican officially confirmed that Pope Francis would release an encyclical on the environment, marking a significant shift in Catholic Church teachings on climate change and sustainability. [Source: CNN](http://edition.cnn.com/2014/02/20/world/pope-francis-encyclical-environment/)
