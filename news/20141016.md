# October 16, 2014

* **Ebola Outbreak**: The World Health Organization (WHO) declared the Ebola outbreak in West Africa to be a Public Health Emergency of International Concern (PHEIC), as the number of cases reached over 7,500 and deaths exceeded 3,000.
* **Malaysia Airlines Flight 370 Disappearance**: One year after Malaysia Airlines Flight 370 vanished, Malaysian authorities announced that they had found the aircraft's black box flight recorders on the ocean floor off the coast of western Australia.
* **US Midterm Elections**: The United States held midterm elections for Congress, with Republicans gaining control of the Senate and Democrats retaining control of the House of Representatives.
* **FIFA World Cup Qualifiers**: In the final round of 2014 FIFA World Cup qualifying matches, Brazil, Argentina, and Colombia secured spots in the tournament, while Mexico qualified via a playoff against New Zealand.
* **Hong Kong Umbrella Movement Protests**: Tens of thousands of pro-democracy protesters took to the streets in Hong Kong, calling for greater autonomy from mainland China and electoral reforms.
* **European Union Immigration Crisis**: The European Union (EU) agreed on a plan to relocate asylum seekers within member states, amid an unprecedented influx of migrants seeking refuge in Europe.
* **Nobel Prizes**: The Nobel Prize in Physics was awarded to Isamu Akasaki, Hiroshi Amano, and Shuji Nakamura for the invention of efficient blue light-emitting diodes (LEDs).
* **NASA's New Horizons Spacecraft**: NASA's New Horizons spacecraft flew by Pluto, providing the first close-up images of the dwarf planet since its discovery in 1930.
* **China's Xi Jinping Visits Europe**: Chinese President Xi Jinping embarked on a six-day trip to Europe, meeting with leaders from France, Germany, and Italy to discuss trade, security, and climate change issues.
* **Floods in South Sudan**: Heavy rainfall caused devastating floods in South Sudan, affecting over 100,000 people and displacing thousands more.
