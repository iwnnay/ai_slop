# Events of November 26, 2014

* **ISIS Claims Responsibility for Beheading of British Aid Worker**: ISIS claimed responsibility for the beheading of Alan Henning, a British aid worker who was kidnapped while on a humanitarian mission in Syria. This was the fourth Western hostage to be killed by ISIS in as many months.
* **Ebola Outbreak Spreads to Guinea's Capital**: The Ebola outbreak that started in West Africa earlier in 2014 continued to spread, with new cases reported in Conakry, Guinea's capital city. The virus had already affected several countries in the region, including Sierra Leone and Liberia.
* **Australian Parliament Passes Same-Sex Marriage Bill**: Australia's parliament passed a bill legalizing same-sex marriage, making it the 15th country to do so. The legislation allowed gay couples to formalize their relationships through a civil ceremony.
* **Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi Visits Pakistan**: Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi made an historic visit to Pakistan, where he met with his Pakistani counterpart Nawaz Sharif and announced plans to increase trade and cultural exchanges between the two countries.
* **Russia's Vladimir Putin Signs Law Expanding Surveillance Capabilities**: Russian President Vladimir Putin signed a law expanding the government's surveillance capabilities, allowing them to monitor internet communications without a court warrant. Critics accused the government of trying to suppress dissent.
* **US Stocks Tumble Amid Concerns Over Global Economy**: The Dow Jones Industrial Average fell by 334 points, or 2.1%, amid concerns over the global economy and the impact of US monetary policy on markets.
* **NBA Player Rajon Rondo Suspended for Physical Altercation**: Boston Celtics point guard Rajon Rondo was suspended for two games without pay after a physical altercation with teammate Jared Sullinger during practice.
* **New York Times Publishes Report on NSA's Global Surveillance Program**: The New York Times published a report revealing details about the National Security Agency's (NSA) global surveillance program, including its ability to collect and analyze massive amounts of data from phone calls and internet communications.
* **Egyptian President Abdel Fattah el-Sisi Wins Presidential Election**: Egyptian President Abdel Fattah el-Sisi won a presidential election with 96.9% of the vote, solidifying his hold on power in the country.
* **NASA's MAVEN Spacecraft Arrives at Mars Orbit**: NASA's Mars Atmosphere and Volatile Evolution (MAVEN) spacecraft arrived at Mars orbit, beginning its mission to study the planet's upper atmosphere and understand how it was lost over time.
