Important Events for 2014-08-27:

Here are 10 major events that occurred on August 27, 2014:

**August 27, 2014**

#1: **Ebola Outbreak Spreads in West Africa**
The Ebola outbreak continues to spread in Guinea, Liberia, and Sierra Leone, with the World Health Organization (WHO) confirming over 2,000 cases.

#2: **Michael Brown Shooting Protest Turns Violent**
Protests erupt in Ferguson, Missouri, following the shooting death of Michael Brown, an unarmed black teenager, by a white police officer. The protests turn violent, resulting in property damage and injuries.

#3: **US-Iran Nuclear Talks Resume**
The US and Iran resume nuclear talks in Geneva, Switzerland, aimed at reaching a deal to curb Tehran's nuclear program.

#4: **UN Security Council Condemns Israeli Settlements**
The United Nations Security Council condemns Israel's settlements in the West Bank, calling them "illegitimate" under international law.

#5: **Australian Prime Minister Tony Abbott Visits China**
Australian Prime Minister Tony Abbott becomes the first Western leader to visit President Xi Jinping at China's new national security headquarters.

#6: **NHL Teams Announce New Scheduling Format**
The National Hockey League (NHL) announces a new scheduling format, featuring more divisional games and realigned conferences.

#7: **FIFA World Cup Qualifying Matches Conclude**
FIFA World Cup qualifying matches conclude in Africa, with Nigeria securing qualification for the tournament in Brazil.

#8: **European Central Bank Announces Stimulus Package**
The European Central Bank (ECB) announces a stimulus package to boost the eurozone economy, including bond-buying and interest rate cuts.

#9: **Hurricane Odile Makes Landfall in Mexico**
Hurricane Odile makes landfall in Baja California, Mexico, causing widespread damage and power outages.

#10: **India's Prime Minister Narendra Modi Visits Japan**
Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi visits Japan, marking the first visit by an Indian prime minister to Japan since 1978. The two countries sign several agreements on trade, security, and technology.
