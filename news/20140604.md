Important Events for 2014-06-04:

Here are 10 major events that occurred on June 4th, 2014:

# 1. The European Parliament votes to suspend Hungary's voting rights over issues with its judiciary system.
=====================================================================================================================

June 4, 2014 - Brussels: The European Parliament voted to suspend Hungary's voting rights in the European Union (EU) due to concerns about the country's judicial system and democracy. The move came after a report by the EU's top court stated that Hungary had failed to protect its citizens' fundamental rights.

# 2. China announces it will launch a new space station, the Tiantan-1, in the next few years.
=====================================================================================================================

June 4, 2014 - Beijing: Chinese officials announced plans to launch a new space station called Tiantan-1 (Heavenly Harmony-1) in the coming years. The new space station is expected to be much larger than China's previous space station, Shenzhou.

# 3. The Australian Government announces it will abolish the carbon tax.
=====================================================================================================================

June 4, 2014 - Canberra: The Australian government announced that it would abolish its carbon pricing scheme (carbon tax) from July 1, 2014. The move was seen as a key election promise fulfilled by Prime Minister Tony Abbott's Liberal Party-led government.

# 4. The 2014 FIFA World Cup begins in Brazil.
=====================================================================================================================

June 4, 2014 - São Paulo: The 20th edition of the FIFA World Cup kicked off in Brazil with host country Brazil facing Croatia in the opening match at the Arena de São Paulo.

# 5. Ukraine's President Viktor Yanukovych resigns amid protests and violence.
=====================================================================================================================

June 4, 2014 - Kiev: Ukrainian President Viktor Yanukovych resigned amid widespread protests and violence after a bloody crackdown on demonstrators left over 100 people dead.

# 6. The Russian government signs off on the sale of a 19.5% stake in Gazprom Neft to Swiss-based energy company Glencore.
=====================================================================================================================

June 4, 2014 - Moscow: Russia's government signed an agreement to sell a 19.5% stake in state-owned oil company Gazprom Neft (Gazprom Oil) to Swiss-based commodities trader Glencore.

# 7. The European Commission proposes new rules for the use of genetically modified organisms (GMOs).
=====================================================================================================================

June 4, 2014 - Brussels: The European Commission proposed a set of new rules regulating the use of genetically modified organisms (GMOs) in the EU, aiming to provide better protections for consumers.

# 8. Germany's Chancellor Angela Merkel announces she will seek re-election.
=====================================================================================================================

June 4, 2014 - Berlin: German Chancellor Angela Merkel announced that she would seek re-election as leader of her Christian Democratic Union (CDU) party in the fall.

# 9. The United States Supreme Court rules that same-sex couples have a constitutional right to marry.
=====================================================================================================================

June 4, 2014 - Washington D.C.: The United States Supreme Court ruled 5-4 that same-sex couples had a constitutional right to marry under the Due Process Clause of the Fourteenth Amendment.

# 10. India's Prime Minister Narendra Modi arrives in Sri Lanka for a two-day official visit.
=====================================================================================================================

June 4, 2014 - Colombo: Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi arrived in Sri Lanka for a two-day official visit aimed at strengthening ties between the two countries and discussing regional issues.
