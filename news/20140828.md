Important Events for 2014-08-28:

Here are 10 significant events that occurred on August 28, 2014:

**August 28, 2014**

#1: Ukraine Crisis Escalates
The Ukrainian military launched an operation to retake the city of Donetsk from pro-Russian separatists, leading to reports of heavy fighting and civilian casualties.

#2: Michael Brown Shooting Anniversary
It was one year since Michael Brown, an unarmed African American teenager, was fatally shot by a police officer in Ferguson, Missouri, sparking widespread protests and calls for police reform.

#3: World War II Veteran Dies at 107
Frank W. Buckles, the last surviving American veteran of World War I and the longest-lived American veteran of both World Wars, passed away at the age of 107.

#4: Gaza Conflict Intensifies
The Israeli military launched a major offensive against Hamas in the Gaza Strip, as tensions continued to escalate amid cross-border rocket attacks and retaliatory airstrikes.

#5: Ebola Outbreak Spreads in West Africa
The World Health Organization (WHO) reported that the Ebola outbreak had spread to four countries in West Africa – Guinea, Liberia, Sierra Leone, and Nigeria – with over 2,000 cases and nearly 1,000 deaths confirmed.

#6: Russian Anti-Gay Laws Cause Global Outcry
Russia's anti-LGBT laws, known as "gay propaganda" laws, were condemned by international leaders and human rights groups, sparking concerns about the impact on gay people in Russia.

#7: Apple Unveils New iPhone
Apple Inc. announced its new iPhone 6 and iPhone 6 Plus smartphones, featuring larger screens, Touch ID fingerprint recognition, and improved cameras.

#8: US-Iran Nuclear Deal Talks Resume
Negotiators from the United States and Iran resumed talks aimed at resolving the long-standing nuclear dispute between the two countries.

#9: India Launches Anti-Corruption Drive
Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi launched a massive anti-corruption drive called "Swachh Bharat Abhiyan" (Clean India Movement), targeting corruption in government and public services.

#10: NFL Player Jason Collins Retires
Jason Collins, the first openly gay athlete to play in one of North America's four major professional sports leagues (the NBA, MLB, NHL, or NFL), announced his retirement from professional basketball.

These are just a few of the significant events that occurred on August 28, 2014.
