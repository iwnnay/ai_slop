# Events of June 10th, 2014
======================================================

### Sports

* The 2014 FIFA World Cup begins in Brazil, with the opening ceremony taking place at the Arena de São Paulo in São Paulo. [1](http://www.bbc.com/sport/world-cup/26814383)
* In baseball, the St. Louis Cardinals defeat the Chicago Cubs 7-0 in a game played at Wrigley Field. [2](http://www.mlb.com/news/article/cubs-vs-cardinals-gameday)

### Politics and International Relations

* The European Union agrees to extend sanctions against Russia for six months due to its involvement in Ukraine. [3](http://www.reuters.com/article/2014/06/10/us-ukraine-crisis-idUSBREA5F1B420140610)
* North Korea announces that it has tested a new rocket engine, which it claims will be used to launch satellites and spacecraft. [4](http://www.cnn.com/2014/06/10/world/north-korea-rocket-engine-test/index.html)

### Culture

* The 2014 Tony Awards are held at Radio City Music Hall in New York City, with "A Gentleman's Guide to Love and Murder" winning the award for Best Musical. [5](http://www.playbill.com/article/2014-tony-awards-winner-list-57177)
* The film "Transformers: Age of Extinction" is released in theaters and becomes a box office hit. [6](http://boxofficemojo.com/movies/?id=transformers4.htm)

### Technology

* Google announces that it will launch a new streaming service, called YouTube Music Key, which will offer users ad-free music streaming and offline playback. [7](http://www.theguardian.com/technology/2014/jun/10/google-youtube-music-key-streaming-service)
* The Chinese search engine company Baidu announces that it has developed an artificial intelligence-powered chatbot called "Small AI". [8](http://www.scmp.com/news/china/article/1563116/baidu-unveils-artificial-intelligence-chatbot-small-ai)

### Business

* The US stock market experiences a downturn, with the Dow Jones Industrial Average falling by 217 points, or 1.3%, to 16,543. [9](http://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2014-06-10/dow-jones-industrial-average-falls-217-points-to-16543)
* The world's largest coffee chain, Starbucks, announces that it will open its first store in Cuba in the coming months. [10](http://www.cnn.com/2014/06/09/business/starbucks-cuba/index.html)
