**2014-12-10 Global Events**

* **Nobel Peace Prize**: Malala Yousafzai and Kailash Satyarthi were awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for their struggle against the suppression of children and young people and for the right of all children to education.
* **Ebola outbreak**: The World Health Organization (WHO) reported that the Ebola virus had spread to 9 countries in West Africa, with a total of over 18,000 cases and more than 8,000 deaths.
* **Ukraine-Russia conflict**: Ukrainian forces captured the key port city of Mariupol from pro-Russian separatists, marking a significant gain for the government in its fight against Russian-backed rebels.
* **NASA's Orion spacecraft**: NASA launched its new Orion spacecraft on an unmanned test flight around Earth, designed to test the ship's systems and prepare it for future missions to deep space.
* **Ebola vaccine**: The WHO announced that an experimental Ebola vaccine had shown promising results in a small clinical trial, with 100% of participants showing no symptoms after receiving the jab.
* **Hong Kong protests**: Thousands of protesters took to the streets of Hong Kong to demand greater autonomy from mainland China, as tensions between pro-democracy activists and the government continued to escalate.
* **FIFA World Cup**: The draw for the 2018 FIFA World Cup took place in St. Petersburg, Russia, with Brazil, Spain, Germany, Italy, and Argentina all entering the tournament as top seeds.
* **South African parliament**: South Africa's parliament voted to impe Zuma, the country's president, following allegations of corruption and abuse of power.
* **Google's Project Ara**: Google announced that it was canceling its modular smartphone project, known as Project Ara, which had been in development for several years.
* **Asteroid flyby**: A small asteroid called 2014 XM3 made a close approach to Earth, passing within about 1.2 million kilometers (750,000 miles) of the planet.
