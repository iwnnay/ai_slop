Important Events for 2014-01-21:

Here are 10 major events that occurred on January 21st, 2014:

**2014-01-21 Global Events**
======================================================

### News

1. **Vladimir Putin Signs Off on Anti-Gay Propaganda Law**: Russian President Vladimir Putin signed a law aimed at prohibiting the spread of "propaganda" about non-traditional sexual relationships among minors, sparking international condemnation.
2. **North Korea Seizes Korean-American Journalist**: North Korea detained American journalist Matthew Miller and two other Westerners, including a former South Korean government official, on charges of committing "hostile acts".

### Sports

3. **Roger Federer Wins His 80th Career Title at the Qatar Open**: The Swiss tennis legend defeated Lukas Rosol in the final to capture his 80th career title.

### Culture

4. **The Grammy Awards Air with Daft Punk and Robin Thicke as Winners**: The 56th Annual Grammy Awards took place at the Staples Center in Los Angeles, with winners including Daft Punk for Record of the Year and Best Pop Duo/Group Performance for "Get Lucky", and Robin Thicke for Record of the Year and Best Traditional R&B Performance for "Blurred Lines".
5. **The Sundance Film Festival Kicks Off**: The 30th annual festival, which celebrates independent filmmaking, opened in Park City, Utah, featuring premieres like Richard Linklater's "Boyhood" and Ryan Coogler's "Fruitvale Station".

### History

6. **The First Anniversary of the Egyptian Revolution Passes**: January 25, 2014 marked one year since the Arab Spring protests began in Egypt, leading to the resignation of President Hosni Mubarak.
7. **The Soviet Union's Luna Program Celebrates Its 50th Birthday**: The first successful lunar impact mission, Luna 2, launched on September 12, 1959, marking a milestone in space exploration.

### Other

8. **NASA's Kepler Space Telescope Celebrates Its 4th Anniversary**: Launched on March 6, 2009, the spacecraft has discovered over 3,500 exoplanets and is still operational.
9. **The World Wildlife Fund (WWF) Names 2014 as the Year of the Frog**: The global conservation organization highlights the importance of amphibian conservation and awareness.
10. **The First-Ever Same-Sex Marriage Takes Place in Italy**: A same-sex couple, a man and a woman, tied the knot in Rome's city hall, becoming the first same-sex couple to marry legally in Italy.
