# Events of July 20, 2014
===============


### International News

* **Malaysia Airlines Flight 17**: Malaysia Airlines Flight MH17, carrying 298 people from Amsterdam to Kuala Lumpur, is shot down by a surface-to-air missile while flying over Ukrainian territory. The incident leads to widespread international outrage and calls for accountability.
* **Israel-Gaza Conflict**: Israeli forces conduct airstrikes on Gaza Strip, killing at least 15 Palestinians, as tensions escalate in the ongoing conflict.

### Sports

* **Tour de France**: French cyclist Christopher Froome wins the Tour de France, his second consecutive title. He completes the grueling three-week event with a time of 82 hours, 56 minutes, and 10 seconds.
* **MLB All-Star Game**: The American League defeats the National League 3-0 in the Major League Baseball All-Star Game at Target Field in Minneapolis, Minnesota.

### Culture

* **Comic-Con International**: The San Diego Comic-Con International, a premier event for comic book enthusiasts and pop culture fans, takes place from July 24 to July 27. This year's attendees include celebrities like Harrison Ford, Jennifer Lawrence, and Stan Lee.
* **Festival d'Été de Québec**: The Quebec City Summer Festival kicks off its 47th edition, featuring a diverse lineup of artists including Arcade Fire, Coldplay, and Pharrell Williams.

### Historical

* **John Paul Stevens Retires**: Associate Justice John Paul Stevens announces his retirement from the United States Supreme Court after serving for nearly 35 years. His departure creates a vacancy that is later filled by Judge Sonia Sotomayor.
* **Soviet Union Dissolves**: On this day in 1990, the Soviet Union officially dissolves into 15 independent countries, marking the end of an era and paving the way for new nations in Eastern Europe.

### Other

* **Weather Extremes**: A heat wave hits the northeastern United States, with temperatures reaching up to 100°F (38°C) in some areas. Meanwhile, heavy rainfall causes flash flooding in parts of Australia.
* **Solar Flare**: A powerful solar flare is released from the sun, affecting Earth's magnetic field and causing aurora displays visible at lower latitudes.
