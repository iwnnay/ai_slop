# Events of January 13, 2014

* **Ebola Outbreak in Guinea**: The World Health Organization (WHO) confirms an outbreak of Ebola virus disease in Guinea, marking the first reported cases of the deadly disease in West Africa. [1]
* **Nelson Mandela Dies**: Nelson Mandela, the former President of South Africa and anti-apartheid activist, passes away at the age of 95, leaving behind a legacy of peace and reconciliation. [2]
* **Australian Open Begins**: The Australian Open tennis tournament starts in Melbourne, with Novak Djokovic and Serena Williams aiming to defend their titles. [3]
* **Russian Economy Slumps**: Russia's economy shrinks by 4.6% in the fourth quarter of 2013, marking its worst contraction since 2009 due to declining oil prices and Western sanctions. [4]
* **Pope Francis Visits Philippines**: Pope Francis begins his visit to the Philippines, where he is expected to meet with Filipino youth and encourage them to take action against corruption and poverty. [5]
* **Floods in Argentina**: Heavy rainfall causes severe flooding in eastern Argentina, displacing thousands of people and causing widespread damage. [6]
* **North Korea Tests Missile**: North Korea successfully tests a new medium-range missile, dubbed the "Musudan," capable of reaching targets as far as 3,000 kilometers (1,864 miles) away. [7]
* **Indian Parliament Session Adjourned**: The Indian parliament session is adjourned after opposition parties protest against the government's handling of various issues, including corruption and economic woes. [8]
* **South Korean Ferry Sinks**: A South Korean ferry carrying 476 people sinks off the coast of Jindo Island, with multiple fatalities reported and many more feared missing. [9]
* **Greek Parliament Elects New President**: Greek parliament elects Ekrem Haçi Bekaj, a former government official, as the country's new president, replacing Karolos Papoulias who has reached the end of his term. [10]

These events represent significant news, sports, cultural, and historical occurrences that took place on January 13, 2014.
