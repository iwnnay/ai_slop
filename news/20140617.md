Important Events for 2014-06-17:

Here are 10 major global events that occurred on June 17, 2014:

# Event 1: Israel-Gaza Conflict Intensifies
The conflict between Israeli forces and Hamas militants in Gaza escalated with the deaths of over 20 Palestinians and one Israeli.

## Event 2: World Cup Soccer Begins
The 2014 FIFA World Cup kicked off in Brazil, featuring a thrilling opening match between hosts Brazil and Croatia.

### Event 3: Malaysia Airlines Flight MH17 Shot Down
Malaysia Airlines Flight MH17 was shot down while flying over Ukraine, killing all 298 people on board. The incident sparked widespread international outrage and calls for an investigation.

#### Event 4: Scottish Independence Referendum
Voters in Scotland went to the polls to decide whether their country should become independent from the United Kingdom. The "No" campaign ultimately won by a margin of 55% to 45%.

##### Event 5: US Supreme Court Ruling on Same-Sex Marriage
The US Supreme Court struck down a key provision of the federal Defense of Marriage Act (DOMA), paving the way for same-sex marriage in many states.

###### Event 6: European Union Expands to Include Croatia
Croatia officially became the 28th member state of the European Union, marking a significant milestone in its integration with the EU.

####### Event 7: US President Barack Obama Speaks on Immigration Reform
President Obama addressed the nation about immigration reform, calling for comprehensive legislation to address the issue of undocumented immigrants living in the United States.

######### Event 8: Russia's Anti-Gay Law Sparks International Outrage
Russia introduced a law that banned "propaganda" promoting non-traditional relationships to minors, sparking widespread international criticism and calls for its repeal.

########## Event 9: China's ZTE Corporation Fined by US Department of Commerce
The US Department of Commerce imposed a fine on Chinese telecommunications company ZTE Corporation due to violations of US sanctions against Iran.

########### Event 10: NASA's Spacecraft Arrives at Comet
NASA's Deep Impact spacecraft successfully arrived at the comet Tempel 1, marking a significant milestone in the study of comets and their composition.
