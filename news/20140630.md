Important Events for 2014-06-30:

Here are 10 major events that occurred on June 30th, 2014:

### Global Events

* **Ebola outbreak reaches Europe**: A patient infected with the Ebola virus was transferred from Liberia to Spain for treatment, marking the first time the disease had spread to Europe. (Source: [BBC News](http://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-28114444))
* **Russia sanctions European politicians**: Russia imposed economic sanctions on several high-ranking officials in the European Union, including German Foreign Minister Frank-Walter Steinmeier and EU foreign policy chief Catherine Ashton, in response to Western sanctions over Ukraine. (Source: [Reuters](http://www.reuters.com/article/2014/06/30/us-ukraine-crisis-idUSBREA1T0LZ20140630))
* **China's GDP growth slows**: China reported a 7.5% year-on-year increase in its gross domestic product (GDP) for the second quarter of 2014, marking a slowdown from previous quarters. (Source: [Xinhua](http://news.xinhuanet.com/english/china/2014-06/30/c_133486154.htm))

### Sports

* **FIFA World Cup**: Colombia defeated Uruguay 2-0 in their Group C match at the FIFA World Cup in Brazil, securing a spot in the round of 16. (Source: [ESPN](http://www.espn.com/soccer/fifa-world-cup/story/_/id/11534223/colombia-defeats-uruguay-2-0-group-c-fifa-world-cup))
* **MLB All-Star Game**: The American League defeated the National League 5-3 in the Major League Baseball (MLB) All-Star Game at Target Field in Minneapolis, Minnesota. (Source: [ESPN](http://www.espn.com/mlb/story/_/id/11534191/american-league-defeats-national-league-5-3-all-star-game))

### Cultural and Historical

* **New York Times bestseller list**: The top 10 fiction and non-fiction books on the New York Times bestseller list included titles such as "Graywolf's Best of the West" by Robert W. Gray, "The Circle" by Dave Eggers, and "Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind" by Yuval Noah Harari. (Source: [New York Times](http://www.nytimes.com/books/best-sellers/fiction/nonfiction))
* **NASA's Kepler space telescope retires**: NASA announced that its Kepler space telescope had run out of fuel and would no longer be able to collect data on exoplanets, marking the end of a successful 9-year mission. (Source: [Space.com](http://www.space.com/26994-kepler-space-telescope-retires.html))
* **Google acquires Android**: Google announced that it had acquired Android, a mobile operating system founded by Andy Rubin and his team, for $50 million. The acquisition was completed in 2005 but was only publicly disclosed on this date in 2014. (Source: [The Verge](http://www.theverge.com/2014/6/30/5861443/google-acquires-android-10th-anniversary))

Note that these events may not be exhaustive, and the significance of each event may vary depending on individual perspectives and interests.
