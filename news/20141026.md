Here are 10 major events that occurred on October 26, 2014:

### October 26, 2014: A Day of Global Significance

* **Ebola Outbreak:** The World Health Organization (WHO) announced that the Ebola outbreak in West Africa had reached a critical phase, with 4,500 cases and 2,200 deaths reported.
* **Gaza Ceasefire:** A ceasefire agreement was reached between Israel and Hamas, ending a seven-week conflict that killed over 2,100 Palestinians and 67 Israelis.
* **Vladimir Putin's Sanctions:** Russia imposed sanctions on Ukraine, including a ban on imports of Ukrainian goods, in response to the country's decision to sign an association agreement with the European Union.
* **Pope Francis' Visit:** Pope Francis arrived in Sri Lanka, marking the first papal visit to the island nation since 1995. He urged reconciliation and peace during his stay.
* **Hong Kong Protests:** The "Umbrella Revolution" protests in Hong Kong entered their third month, with pro-democracy activists occupying major roads and demanding greater autonomy from China.
* **Russian Warplane Incident:** A Russian Su-27 fighter jet intercepted a US Air Force RC-135 reconnaissance plane over the Black Sea, prompting concern about the escalation of tensions between Russia and NATO.
* **European Parliament Elections:** European parliamentary elections took place in 28 countries, with gains for anti-EU parties such as UKIP and Front National.
* **Cuba-US Relations:** The United States and Cuba announced that they would re-establish diplomatic relations after more than five decades, with the US easing travel restrictions to the island nation.
* **Nobel Prize in Physics:** The Nobel Prize in Physics was awarded to Isamu Akasaki, Hiroshi Amano, and Shuji Nakamura for their invention of efficient blue light-emitting diodes (LEDs).
* **FIFA World Cup Qualifiers:** In international football, several teams qualified for the 2018 FIFA World Cup, including Mexico, Costa Rica, and Honduras in CONCACAF, and Poland and Greece in UEFA.

These events represent significant global developments across politics, health, diplomacy, sports, and culture.
