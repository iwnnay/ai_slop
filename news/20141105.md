Important Events for 2014-11-05:

Here are the top 10 global events that occurred on November 5th, 2014:

# **Ebola Outbreak Update**: The World Health Organization (WHO) reported that the Ebola outbreak in West Africa had spread to nine countries, with 13,000 cases and 7,000 deaths as of November 5th.

# **Islamic State Advances in Iraq**: Islamic State fighters seized control of the Iraqi city of Al-Shurah, bringing their territorial gains to within a few miles of Baghdad.

# **US Midterm Elections**: The United States held its midterm elections for the House of Representatives and one-third of the Senate. Republicans won control of the Senate, while Democrats maintained control of the House.

# **Ebola Vaccine Trial Begins**: A clinical trial began in Sierra Leone to test an Ebola vaccine developed by GlaxoSmithKline (GSK) and the National Institute of Allergy and Infectious Diseases (NIAID).

# **Pope Francis Visits Sri Lanka**: Pope Francis arrived in Sri Lanka for a four-day visit, where he was greeted with cheers and applause.

# **Russian Airliner Crashes**: A Russian airliner, Metrojet Flight 9268, crashed in Egypt's Sinai Peninsula, killing all 224 people on board. The crash was attributed to a terrorist attack.

# **FIFA World Cup Qualifying Matches**: In international soccer news, several countries played crucial FIFA World Cup qualifying matches, including Argentina vs. Peru and Germany vs. Georgia.

# **India Bus Fire Kills Over 20**: A bus fire in the Indian state of Telangana killed at least 24 people and injured many more.

# **Chinese Government Shuffles Cabinet**: The Chinese government announced a major cabinet reshuffle, with several key officials replaced or promoted.

# **NASA's Orion Spacecraft Test**: NASA conducted a successful test flight of its new Orion spacecraft, simulating the conditions for an asteroid mission in 2020.
