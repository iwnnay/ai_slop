# Events of February 5, 2014


1. **Russian Annexation of Crimea**: Russia formally annexed the Ukrainian region of Crimea, following a referendum in which the majority of voters backed secession from Ukraine and reunification with Russia.
2. **Pakistan Taliban Leader Killed**: Hakimullah Mehsud, the leader of the Pakistani Taliban (TTP), was killed in a US drone strike in North Waziristan, Pakistan.
3. **US Weather: Blizzard Warning Issued**: The National Weather Service issued a blizzard warning for the Midwest and Northeast United States, as a powerful winter storm brought heavy snow and strong winds to the region.
4. **Sri Lankan President Resigns**: Sri Lankan President Mahinda Rajapaksa resigned amid protests and allegations of corruption, marking the end of his 10-year rule.
5. **Egyptian Presidential Election**: Egyptians began voting in a presidential election between incumbent President Abdel Fattah el-Sisi and liberal candidate Hamdeen Sabahi.
6. **North Korea Missile Test**: North Korea test-fired three ballistic missiles into the sea off its eastern coast, according to South Korean military officials.
7. **Australian Open Tennis Tournament**: Rafael Nadal won his fifth Australian Open title by defeating Stan Wawrinka in the men's singles final.
8. **NASA's Mars Rover Discovery**: NASA scientists announced that their Curiosity rover had discovered evidence of ancient lakes and rivers on Mars, providing insights into the planet's watery past.
9. **South African Archbishop Tutu Dies**: Desmond Tutu, the Nobel Peace Prize-winning Anglican cleric who was a leading advocate for racial equality in South Africa, passed away at the age of 90.
10. **Apple Pay Launches**: Apple launched its mobile payment system, Apple Pay, allowing users to make purchases using their iPhones and Apple Watches.
