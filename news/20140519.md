Important Events for 2014-05-19:

Here are 10 major events that occurred on May 19, 2014:

**Events of Note**
==================

### 1. Malaysia Airlines Flight 370 Search Efforts Intensify

The search for Malaysia Airlines Flight 370, which went missing on March 8, 2014, intensified on this day with the deployment of a remote-operated vehicle (ROV) to the seabed in the Indian Ocean.

### 2. United States Secretly Built Stuxnet Computer Worm

It was revealed that the United States and Israel had secretly developed and deployed the Stuxnet computer worm, which targeted Iran's nuclear facilities, as part of Operation Olympic Games.

### 3. European Parliament Approves EU-US Free Trade Deal

The European Parliament approved a free trade agreement between the European Union and the United States, known as the Transatlantic Trade and Investment Partnership (TTIP).

### 4. Ukrainian Pro-Russian Separatists Seize Government Buildings

Pro-Russian separatists in Ukraine seized government buildings in several cities, including Donetsk and Lugansk, sparking concerns of a full-scale conflict.

### 5. Google Acquires Nest for $3.2 Billion

Google announced that it had acquired home automation company Nest for $3.2 billion, marking one of the largest acquisitions in the company's history.

### 6. NBA Playoffs: Indiana Pacers Defeat Washington Wizards

In Game 7 of their first-round playoff series, the Indiana Pacers defeated the Washington Wizards 80-69 to advance to the second round.

### 7. Google Launches Android L Operating System

Google announced the launch of its new Android L operating system, which featured significant improvements in performance and user experience.

### 8. New York City Mayor Bill de Blasio Outlines Education Plan

New York City Mayor Bill de Blasio outlined his education plan, which included proposals to increase funding for public schools and implement a universal pre-K program.

### 9. NASA's Orion Spacecraft Completes First Test Flight

NASA's Orion spacecraft completed its first test flight, known as Exploration Flight Test-1 (EFT-1), as part of the agency's plans to return humans to the Moon by 2020.

### 10. American Cancer Society Announces New Guidelines for Breast Cancer Screening

The American Cancer Society announced new guidelines for breast cancer screening, recommending that women at average risk should begin annual mammograms at age 40 rather than 50.
