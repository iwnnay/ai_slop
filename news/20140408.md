Here are 10 major global events that occurred on April 8, 2014:

# April 8, 2014: A Day of Significant Events
=====================================================

### 1. Russian Intervention in Ukraine Begins

The Ukrainian government accused Russia of launching a military intervention in eastern Ukraine, with reports of Russian troops and tanks entering the country. This marked the beginning of Russia's involvement in the Ukrainian conflict.

### 2. Boston Marathon Bombing Suspect Sentenced

Dzhokhar Tsarnaev, one of the brothers responsible for the Boston Marathon bombing that killed three people and injured hundreds more, was sentenced to death. The sentencing came after a jury found him guilty of all 30 counts related to the attack.

### 3. Malaysia Airlines Flight MH370 Disappearance Mystery Deepens

The search for missing Malaysia Airlines Flight MH370 continued with no sign of the plane or its 239 passengers and crew. The investigation remained shrouded in mystery, with theories ranging from deliberate hijacking to mechanical failure.

### 4. Google Buys Nest Labs

Google acquired Nest Labs, a company known for its smart thermostats and smoke detectors, in an effort to expand its presence in the Internet of Things (IoT) market.

### 5. Ebola Outbreak Spreads in West Africa

The Ebola outbreak in Guinea, Liberia, and Sierra Leone continued to spread, with cases rising sharply. The World Health Organization (WHO) declared it a public health emergency of international concern.

### 6. European Union Approves New Sanctions on Russia

The European Union approved new sanctions against Russian officials involved in the Ukrainian crisis, including freezing assets and travel bans. This move was seen as a response to Russia's continued involvement in Ukraine.

### 7. NASA's Kepler Space Telescope Retires

NASA announced that its Kepler space telescope had completed its primary mission, having discovered thousands of exoplanets during its four years in operation. The telescope would continue to collect data until its fuel ran out.

### 8. New York Yankees Sign Chase Headley

The New York Yankees signed free agent third baseman Chase Headley to a four-year contract, marking the team's latest move to bolster their roster for the upcoming season.

### 9. Turkish Protesters Defy Government Crackdown

Protests in Turkey continued despite a government crackdown on demonstrations calling for greater freedom and democracy. The protests were sparked by a proposal to develop Gezi Park in Istanbul.

### 10. China's Alibaba Group Goes Public

Alibaba Group, one of the world's largest e-commerce companies, went public with an initial public offering (IPO) that raised $25 billion, making it the largest IPO in history at the time.

These events provide a glimpse into the significant news and developments from around the world on April 8, 2014.
