# Major Global Events of August 13, 2014


* **Russia-Ukraine Conflict**: Russian-backed separatists in Ukraine's Donetsk region declare independence from Kiev, sparking international concern over the escalating crisis.
* **Ebola Outbreak**: The World Health Organization (WHO) reports a significant increase in Ebola cases in West Africa, with Guinea, Liberia, and Sierra Leone affected. The outbreak would eventually claim thousands of lives.
* **Israel-Gaza Conflict**: Israeli forces strike a United Nations-run school in Gaza City, killing at least 10 people and injuring many more. The conflict had already claimed hundreds of lives by this point.
* **ISIS Offensives**: ISIS militants launch a series of attacks on Iraqi cities, including the capture of the city of Mosul, which would become one of the group's key strongholds.
* **Floods in India**: Heavy rainfall and flash flooding in India's state of Bihar leave at least 100 people dead and thousands displaced.
* **South Africa Unrest**: Protests erupt in South Africa over allegations of police brutality and corruption, leading to widespread violence and property damage.
* **China's First Manned Space Station**: China launches its first space station, the Tiantan-1, which will serve as a research facility for scientists.
* **Brazil World Cup**: In the 2014 FIFA World Cup, Brazil is eliminated from the tournament after losing to Germany in the semifinals. The match would be remembered for Germany's devastating 7-1 victory and Neymar's injury.
* **European Heat Wave**: A severe heat wave hits Europe, with temperatures reaching as high as 45°C (113°F) in some areas. The heat wave would eventually claim hundreds of lives across the continent.
* **CERN Discovery**: Scientists at CERN's Large Hadron Collider announce the discovery of a new particle that could help explain dark matter and dark energy.

These events represent just a few of the significant global happenings on August 13, 2014.
