# Events of October 8, 2014
=====================================

### Politics and Governance

1. **Russian Anti-Gay Law Update**: Russian authorities announced plans to strengthen enforcement of the country's controversial "gay propaganda" law, which bans promoting homosexuality to minors.

### Sports

2. **NFL: Tom Brady Sets Record**: New England Patriots quarterback Tom Brady set a new record for most career touchdown passes in regular season games (354) as his team defeated the Minnesota Vikings 16-10.
3. **MLB Playoffs: Giants Defeat Pirates**: The San Francisco Giants defeated the Pittsburgh Pirates 8-0 in Game 1 of their National League Division Series.

### Technology and Science

4. **SpaceX Launch**: SpaceX successfully launched its Falcon 9 rocket from Cape Canaveral, Florida, carrying a batch of satellites into orbit.
5. **NASA's Mars Orbiter**: NASA's Mars Atmosphere and Volatile Evolution (MAVEN) spacecraft successfully entered Mars' orbit to study the planet's atmosphere.

### Health and Medicine

6. **Ebola Outbreak Update**: The World Health Organization (WHO) reported 4,000 new cases of Ebola in West Africa over the past week, bringing the total number of cases to over 7,500.
7. **H1N1 Vaccine Distribution**: The WHO distributed more than 100 million doses of H1N1 influenza vaccine to countries worldwide.

### Economy and Business

8. **European Economy Shrinks**: The European Union's economy contracted by 0.2% in the third quarter of 2014, its first recession since 2009.
9. **Gold Price Drop**: Gold prices fell to a six-month low as investors sold off their positions due to concerns about interest rates and economic growth.

### Culture and Entertainment

10. **Movie Release: "Gone Girl" Debuts**: David Fincher's thriller film "Gone Girl," based on Gillian Flynn's best-selling novel, was released in theaters worldwide, starring Ben Affleck and Rosamund Pike.
