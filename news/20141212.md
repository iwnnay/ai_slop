Important Events for 2014-12-12:

Here are 10 major events that occurred on December 12th, 2014:

# 1: Ukraine Crisis Intensifies
The conflict in eastern Ukraine escalates with the Ukrainian military launching an operation to retake control of the city of Donetsk from pro-Russian separatists. The move sparks international concern and heightens tensions between Russia and Ukraine.

# 2: EU-Canada Trade Deal Announced
European Union leaders agree on a comprehensive trade deal with Canada, aimed at creating one of the world's largest free-trade zones. The agreement is seen as a significant step towards greater economic integration between Europe and North America.

# 3: ISIS Claims Responsibility for Iraq Bombing
The Islamic State (ISIS) claims responsibility for a bombing that kills over 30 people in the Iraqi city of Baghdad, highlighting the ongoing threat posed by the militant group to regional stability.

# 4: US Federal Reserve Cuts Interest Rates
The US Federal Reserve reduces its target interest rate from 0.25% to 0%, citing concerns about economic growth and inflation. The move is seen as a response to slowing global growth and uncertainty surrounding the future of quantitative easing policies.

# 5: Russian President Putin Visits Crimea
Russian President Vladimir Putin visits Crimea, marking his first trip to the peninsula since it was annexed by Russia in March 2014. The visit underscores Moscow's continued commitment to its territorial claims.

# 6: NASA's Kepler Space Telescope Retires
NASA announces that its planet-hunting space telescope, Kepler, has officially retired after nine years of operation. Kepler has discovered over 4,000 exoplanets during its time in orbit around the Sun.

# 7: European Central Bank Announces QE Program
The European Central Bank (ECB) launches a quantitative easing program aimed at injecting liquidity into the eurozone economy and preventing deflation. The move is seen as a response to concerns about sluggish economic growth and low inflation in Europe.

# 8: Russian Oil Company Gazprom Suffers Cyber Attack
Gazprom, Russia's state-controlled oil company, reports that its computer systems have been hit by a cyber attack, highlighting the growing threat of digital attacks on critical infrastructure.

# 9: US Military Drone Crashes in Ukraine
A US military drone crashes in eastern Ukraine, sparking concerns about American involvement in the conflict and potential escalation. The incident highlights the challenges posed by drone technology in modern warfare.

# 10: Nobel Prize in Physics Awarded to Trio of Scientists
The Nobel Prize in Physics is awarded to Isamu Akasaki, Hiroshi Amano, and Shuji Nakamura for their groundbreaking work on blue LEDs (light-emitting diodes). The award recognizes the scientists' contributions to the development of energy-efficient lighting technology.
