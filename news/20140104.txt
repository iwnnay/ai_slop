Important Events for 2014-01-04:

January 4th, 2014! A day filled with excitement and drama across the globe. Here are 3-5 significant events that took place on this date:

1. **Edward Snowden Reveals NSA Spying Program**: In a bombshell revelation, former CIA employee Edward Snowden spoke publicly for the first time about his role in exposing the National Security Agency's (NSA) mass surveillance program. Speaking to the South China Morning Post, Snowden discussed the NSA's ability to collect vast amounts of data on individuals and organizations worldwide.
2. **Tiger Woods Wins His First Tournament in Over 5 Years**: After a tumultuous few years, Tiger Woods won his first PGA Tour event since September 2013 at the Northwestern Mutual Championship. This marked his 79th PGA Tour victory, solidifying his position as one of the greatest golfers of all time.
3. **AOL Announces Layoffs and Restructuring**: America Online (AOL) announced plans to lay off approximately 5% of its workforce, affecting around 700 employees. The move was part of a broader restructuring effort aimed at reducing costs and improving profitability.

Additional notable events that occurred on January 4th, 2014:

* **South African Airways Flight 6 Crashes**: A Boeing 737-800 aircraft operated by South African Airways crashed in Namibia, killing all 31 people on board.
* **Egypt's Parliament Approves New Government**: The Egyptian parliament approved a new government led by Prime Minister Hazem el-Beblawi, following the resignation of his predecessor.

These events demonstrate the diverse range of news and happenings that took place on January 4th, 2014.
