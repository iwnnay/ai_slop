# November 13, 2014
===============

1. **Paris Attacks**: A series of coordinated terrorist attacks occur in Paris, France, targeting the satirical newspaper Charlie Hebdo and a kosher market. The attacks leave at least 17 people dead and many more injured.
2. **Nobel Prize**: The Nobel Prize in Physics is awarded to Isamu Akasaki, Hiroshi Amano, and Shuji Nakamura for their invention of efficient blue light-emitting diodes (LEDs).
3. **Ebola Outbreak**: The World Health Organization announces that the Ebola outbreak has reached 28 countries, making it the largest ever recorded.
4. **Russian-Georgian Border Dispute**: Tensions rise along the Russia-Georgia border after a Georgian soldier is killed in an exchange of fire between Russian and Georgian forces.
5. **Migrant Crisis**: The European Union launches an emergency meeting to address the growing migrant crisis, with thousands of people fleeing war-torn countries seeking asylum in Europe.
6. **NASA's Orion Spacecraft**: NASA successfully tests its Orion spacecraft, designed for deep space missions, by launching it into the atmosphere and recovering it safely.
7. **World Cup Qualifying**: In international soccer news, Argentina qualifies for the 2015 Copa America with a 2-1 victory over Ecuador.
8. **Hurricane Patricia**: The National Hurricane Center issues a warning about Hurricane Patricia, which would later become the strongest hurricane ever recorded in Eastern Pacific history.
9. **Ebola Vaccine Trial**: The World Health Organization announces that a vaccine trial has begun to combat the Ebola outbreak, with 3,000 people expected to participate.
10. **NASA's New Horizons Spacecraft**: NASA's New Horizons spacecraft sends back stunning images of Pluto and its moons, marking a major milestone in the exploration of our solar system.
