Important Events for 2014-02-21:

Here are 10 major global events that occurred on February 21, 2014:

# January 29, 2014
### Ukrainian protests escalate

Protesters in Ukraine clash with riot police in the capital city of Kiev, marking a turning point in the ongoing political crisis. The protests began as a response to President Viktor Yanukovych's decision not to sign an EU trade agreement.

## February 21, 2014
### Crimea declares independence from Ukraine

The Crimean Parliament votes to declare independence from Ukraine and become a sovereign state. This move is widely seen as a precursor to Russia's annexation of Crimea in March 2014.

# Other notable events on this date
### Apple buys Beats Electronics for $3 billion

Apple announces that it has acquired Beats Electronics, a headphones and music streaming company founded by rapper Dr. Dre and music producer Jimmy Iovine, for $3 billion. The acquisition expands Apple's presence in the music industry.

### NASA's Mars rover discovers evidence of ancient lake on Mars

NASA's Curiosity rover finds evidence of an ancient lake on Mars that existed about 3.5 billion years ago. The discovery provides insights into the planet's past habitability and the potential for life to exist on Mars.

### Australian Open tennis tournament begins

The 2014 Australian Open, one of the four Grand Slam tennis tournaments, starts at Melbourne Park in Australia. The event features top players from around the world competing for the coveted championship title.

### Winter storms hit the United States

A series of winter storms hits the eastern United States, causing widespread power outages and disrupting transportation systems. The storms dump significant snowfall in areas such as Washington D.C., Philadelphia, and New York City.

### European Union announces new sanctions against Russia

The European Union imposes new economic sanctions on Russia in response to its annexation of Crimea. The sanctions target Russian officials involved in the crisis and restrict access to EU financial markets for certain Russian companies.

### Canadian ice hockey player Patrick Roy is inducted into the Hockey Hall of Fame

Patrick Roy, a legendary Canadian ice hockey player and goalie, is inducted into the Hockey Hall of Fame in Toronto. Roy won four Stanley Cup championships during his playing career with the Montreal Canadiens and Colorado Avalanche.

### The first Ebola outbreak in Guinea occurs

The World Health Organization (WHO) reports that an Ebola virus outbreak has occurred in Guinea, marking the first time the disease has been detected in the West African country. The outbreak eventually spreads to neighboring countries, including Liberia and Sierra Leone.

### The United States Senate confirms Gina McCarthy as EPA administrator

Gina McCarthy is confirmed by the U.S. Senate as the Administrator of the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), becoming the third woman to hold the position. McCarthy has been a key player in implementing environmental policies during the Obama administration.
