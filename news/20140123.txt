Important Events for 2014-01-23:

On January 23rd, 2014, several notable events took place around the world. Here are 3-5 major events that occurred on this date:

1. **North Korea executes high-ranking officials**: North Korean state media announced that Jang Song-thaek, the uncle and former father-in-law of leader Kim Jong-un, had been executed for treason. Jang was a powerful figure in North Korea's government and economy, and his removal marked a significant shift in power dynamics within the country.
2. **FIFA announces 2014 World Cup venues**: The Fédération Internationale de Football Association (FIFA) announced the 12 cities that would host matches during the 2014 FIFA World Cup in Brazil. The cities included Rio de Janeiro, São Paulo, Belo Horizonte, Porto Alegre, and others.
3. **Pope Francis prays for peace**: Pope Francis visited the Israeli Holocaust museum Yad Vashem and prayed for peace in the Middle East during his visit to Israel. This was a significant event, as it marked the first time a pope had visited the site since its establishment in 1965.

* **Russian President Vladimir Putin signs anti-LGBTQ+ law**: Putin signed a law banning LGBTQ+ propaganda, which effectively outlawed any public display of LGBTQ+ identity or expression.
* **Thai protests continue**: Anti-government protesters in Thailand continued their demonstrations, calling for the resignation of Prime Minister Yingluck Shinawatra. The protests ultimately led to a military coup and the ousting of Shinawatra.
