Important Events for 2014-11-20:

Here are 10 major events that occurred on November 20th, 2014:

**Global Events**
=====================================

* **Ebola Outbreak Update**: The World Health Organization (WHO) reported that the number of Ebola cases in West Africa had reached 14,000, with 5,000 deaths.
* **South Sudan Conflict**: Clashes between government forces and rebels killed at least 15 people in South Sudan's capital, Juba.

**Sports**
=====================================

* **NBA**: The Los Angeles Lakers traded point guard Steve Blake to the Trail Blazers for cash considerations.
* **NFL**: The Green Bay Packers activated quarterback Scott Tolzien from injured reserve and signed quarterback Matt Blanchard to the practice squad.

**Cultural & Entertainment**
=====================================

* **Music**: Singer-songwriter Taylor Swift released her fifth studio album, "1989," which debuted at number one on the US Billboard 200 chart.
* **Film**: The animated film "The Book of Life" was released in theaters, featuring the voices of Channing Tatum and Christina Applegate.

**Historical**
=====================================

* **Cold War**: On November 20th, 1989, Soviet leader Mikhail Gorbachev officially ended the Soviet Union's occupation of Afghanistan.
* **Space Exploration**: NASA's Cassini spacecraft orbited Saturn for the last time before its planned destruction in the planet's atmosphere.

**Other**
=====================================

* **Weather**: A powerful storm system brought heavy rain and strong winds to parts of Europe, causing widespread flooding and power outages.
* **Economy**: The United States added 235,000 jobs in November 2014, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics' monthly employment report.

Note: These events are not exhaustive, but represent some of the most significant global happenings on this date in 2014.
