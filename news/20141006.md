Important Events for 2014-10-06:

Here are 10 major events that occurred on October 6, 2014:

# Event 1: Russia-Ukraine Conflict
===============

October 6, 2014 - Pro-Russian separatists in Ukraine's Donetsk region claimed to have shot down two Ukrainian Sukhoi Su-25 fighter jets. The conflict between Ukraine and Russian-backed separatists had been escalating since April.

# Event 2: Ebola Outbreak Update
==================

October 6, 2014 - The World Health Organization (WHO) announced that the Ebola outbreak in West Africa had reached a total of 7,492 cases and 3,091 deaths. The WHO also reported that the number of new cases was declining in Guinea.

# Event 3: Scottish Independence Referendum
=====================

October 6, 2014 - Scotland's First Minister Alex Salmond resigned as leader of the Scottish National Party (SNP) after the country voted to remain part of the United Kingdom. The referendum had been held on September 18, with "No" votes winning by a margin of about 55-45%.

# Event 4: Hong Kong Pro-Democracy Protesters Arrested
=====================

October 6, 2014 - Hong Kong police arrested over 500 protesters who were demanding greater democratic freedoms. The protests had been ongoing since September.

# Event 5: NASA's MAVEN Spacecraft Enters Mars Orbit
=====================

October 6, 2014 - NASA's Mars Atmosphere and Volatile Evolution (MAVEN) spacecraft entered into orbit around Mars. The mission was designed to study the Martian upper atmosphere and how it interacts with the solar wind.

# Event 6: US Presidential Election Update
=====================

October 6, 2014 - In the US presidential election campaign, Republican candidate Donald Trump announced that he would not be running for president. Democrat Hillary Clinton had been leading in most polls at this point.

# Event 7: Nobel Prize in Literature Awarded to French Writer Patrick Modiano
=====================

October 6, 2014 - The Swedish Academy awarded the Nobel Prize in Literature to French writer Patrick Modiano "for the art of memory with which he has evoked the most ungraspable human destinies and uncovered the life-worlds of others."
