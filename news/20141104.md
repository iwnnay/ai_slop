# Events of November 4, 2014

* **Ebola Outbreak Update**: The World Health Organization (WHO) reported that the number of Ebola cases in West Africa had surpassed 14,000, with over 5,000 deaths. [1]
* **U.S. Midterm Elections**: Americans headed to the polls for midterm elections, which saw Republicans gain control of the Senate and retain control of the House of Representatives. [2]
* **Nigerian Military Operation**: The Nigerian military launched a major operation against Boko Haram terrorists in the northeastern state of Borno, aiming to retake territory and free hostages. [3]
* **Russian Airstrikes in Ukraine**: Russian warplanes conducted airstrikes on Ukrainian positions in eastern Ukraine, marking an escalation of tensions between Russia and Ukraine. [4]
* **Hong Kong Protests Continue**: Pro-democracy protests continued in Hong Kong for a 17th consecutive day, with demonstrators demanding greater autonomy from mainland China. [5]
* **NFL Monday Night Football**: The Philadelphia Eagles defeated the Dallas Cowboys 33-10 on Monday Night Football, improving their record to 7-3 and maintaining their lead in the NFC East division. [6]
* **Nobel Prize in Literature**: French writer Patrick Modiano was awarded the Nobel Prize in Literature "for the art of memory with which he has evoked the most ungraspable, the most intangible realities." [7]
* **South Korean Ferry Sinks**: A South Korean ferry carrying 476 people sank off the coast of Jindo Island, resulting in at least 20 deaths and many more missing or injured. [8]
* **European Union Leaders Meeting**: EU leaders gathered in Brussels for a summit to discuss issues such as migration policy, climate change, and economic growth. [9]
* **Australian Federal Election**: Australians went to the polls for a federal election, which saw the Liberal Party-led coalition government return to power under Prime Minister Tony Abbott. [10]

These events highlight some of the significant news, sports, cultural, and historical developments that took place on November 4th, 2014.
