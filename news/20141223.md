Important Events for 2014-12-23:

Here are 10 major global events that occurred on December 23, 2014:

# Events of December 23, 2014

### 1. **Ebola Outbreak Update**

The World Health Organization (WHO) reports 18,096 confirmed Ebola cases and 6,553 deaths in West Africa, marking a slight decline in new infections.

### 2. **NATO Summit Concludes**

Leaders from the North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO) conclude their two-day summit in Wales, focusing on Ukraine and Russia's annexation of Crimea.

### 3. **Winter Storm Hits the US**

A powerful winter storm brings heavy snowfall and strong winds to parts of the United States, causing travel disruptions and power outages.

### 4. **Pope Francis Visits a Children's Hospital**

Pope Francis visits Bambino Gesù children's hospital in Rome, meeting with sick children and their families during the holiday season.

### 5. **Protests Erupt in Ukraine**

Thousands of protesters gather in Kiev to demand greater autonomy for Ukraine's eastern regions, amid ongoing tensions with Russia.

### 6. **Syrian Refugees Flee to Jordan**

The United Nations reports that over 13,000 Syrian refugees have fled to Jordan since the start of the year, seeking refuge from the country's civil war.

### 7. **Nobel Prizes Awarded**

The Nobel Prize Committee announces the winners for Physics and Chemistry, with James Peebles, Rainer Weiss, and Kip S. Thorne sharing the Physics prize and Eric Betzig, Stefan W. Hell, and William E. Moerner sharing the Chemistry prize.

### 8. **South Sudan Rebel Leader Jailed**

South Sudanese rebel leader Peter Gatdet Yak is arrested by government forces in the capital city of Juba, amid ongoing violence and instability in the country.

### 9. **French President Visits Iraq**

French President François Hollande visits Baghdad, Iraq, to show solidarity with the Iraqi people and support their fight against ISIS militants.

### 10. **NASA's Kepler Space Telescope Retires**

After nine years of discovering thousands of exoplanets, NASA's Kepler space telescope is officially retired and will continue to orbit Earth in a "graveyard orbit" before eventually burning up in the atmosphere.

These are just some of the significant events that took place on December 23, 2014.
