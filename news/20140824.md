# **Ebola Outbreak in West Africa**

The Islamic State (ISIS) captured the Syrian town of Qaryatayn, a strategic location near the Lebanese border. The town had previously been held by government forces and was seen as an important stronghold against ISIS.

NASA's Lunar Atmosphere and Dust Environment Explorer (LADEE) spacecraft crashed into the moon's surface after completing its mission to study the lunar atmosphere. LADEE was launched in September 2013 and provided valuable data on the moon's thin exosphere.

# **Tropical Storm Warnings Issued**

# **South Sudan Ceasefire Agreement**
The South Sudanese government and rebel forces signed a ceasefire agreement to end the conflict that had been raging since December 2013. The agreement aimed to establish a permanent cessation of hostilities and pave the way for peace talks.

# **Hong Kong Pro-Democracy Rally**
Thousands of pro-democracy activists took to the streets in Hong Kong to protest against the Chinese government's restrictions on voting rights in the territory. The rally was one of the largest since the Umbrella Movement protests in 2014.

# **Egyptian Military Crackdown on Protesters**
Security forces in Egypt cracked down on protesters demanding an end to President Abdel Fattah el-Sisi's regime, leaving several people injured and hundreds arrested. The crackdown followed a series of protests calling for greater democracy and human rights in the country.
