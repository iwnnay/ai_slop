# October 23, 2014: Major Events of the Day
=====================================

October 23, 2014 was marked by several significant events across various fields. Here are some of the most notable ones:

### 1. Ebola Outbreak Update

The World Health Organization (WHO) reported that a total of 9,941 cases and 4,877 deaths had been recorded in West Africa as of October 23, 2014, making it one of the worst Ebola outbreaks in history.

### 2. Russian Military Build-Up in Ukraine

Russian President Vladimir Putin ordered military troops to enter Ukrainian territory without permission, sparking international outrage and concerns over a full-scale invasion.

### 3. Hong Kong Protests Continue

Pro-democracy protests in Hong Kong entered their eighth week on October 23, with thousands of protesters demanding greater autonomy from China.

### 4. NASA's Orion Spacecraft Test Flights

NASA conducted test flights of its new Orion spacecraft at the Kennedy Space Center in Florida, marking a significant step towards human missions to deep space in the near future.

### 5. FIFA Corruption Scandal Unfolds

FIFA President Sepp Blatter announced that he would not seek re-election as head of world football's governing body amid growing corruption allegations.

### 6. Apple iPhone 6 and Apple Watch Launch

Apple Inc. launched its highly anticipated iPhone 6 smartphone and Apple Watch wearable device, which quickly became a global phenomenon.

### 7. Ebola Vaccine Trial Launched

The World Health Organization (WHO) launched the first large-scale trial of an experimental Ebola vaccine in Guinea, aiming to combat the deadly virus outbreak.

### 8. European Union Migrant Crisis Deepens

The European Union faced growing criticism over its handling of a migrant crisis, with thousands of asylum seekers arriving daily on Greek and Italian shores.

### 9. Nobel Prize in Physics Awarded

The Nobel Prize in Physics was awarded to three researchers - Isamu Akasaki, Hiroshi Amano, and Shuji Nakamura - for the development of blue light-emitting diodes (LEDs).

### 10. Russian Cosmonauts Return from Space Station

Russian cosmonauts Mikhail Kornienko and Scott Kelly returned from a six-month stay on the International Space Station, marking one of the longest continuous space missions in history.

These events represent just a few of the significant news stories, scientific breakthroughs, cultural milestones, and historical happenings that took place on October 23, 2014.
