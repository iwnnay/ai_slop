# January 31st, 2014: Significant Global Events
======================================================

### 1. Ukraine Protests Intensify

* Anti-government protesters in Ukraine clash with police in Kiev, as tensions escalate over the government's decision to suspend preparations for a trade deal with the European Union.

### 2. Sochi Winter Olympics Begin

* The XXII Olympic Winter Games open in Sochi, Russia, featuring athletes from around the world competing in events such as alpine skiing, figure skating, and snowboarding.
{:.no_toc}

### 3. Syrian Warplanes Bomb ISIS Targets

* The Syrian government launches airstrikes against Islamic State (ISIS) targets in the city of Raqqa, marking a shift in its strategy to confront the extremist group.

### 4. US Unemployment Rate Drops

* The Bureau of Labor Statistics reports that the US unemployment rate falls to 6.7%, the lowest level since October 2008.

### 5. Apple Reports Strong Q1 Earnings

* Technology giant Apple announces strong first-quarter earnings, driven by record sales of its iPhone and iPad products.

### 6. Egypt's Morsi Receives Life Sentence

* Former Egyptian President Mohamed Morsi is sentenced to life in prison for his role in the death of protesters during demonstrations against his rule.

### 7. Russia Cracks Down on Protesters

* Russian authorities arrest several prominent anti-government activists, including opposition leader Alexei Navalny, as part of a wider crackdown on dissent.

### 8. Nobel Peace Prize Winner Malala Yousafzai Speaks at Oxford University

* Malala Yousafzai, the Pakistani activist who survived a Taliban assassination attempt and won the Nobel Peace Prize in 2014, delivers a speech at Oxford University, emphasizing the importance of education for girls.

### 9. FIFA Bans Qatari Football Official

* The International Federation of Association Football (FIFA) bans Qatari football official Mohammed Bin Hammam from all football-related activities due to allegations of corruption.

### 10. New Species of Ancient Human Discovered in Philippines

* Scientists announce the discovery of a new species of ancient human, Homo luzonensis, found in the Philippines and dating back around 50,000-67,000 years.
