Important Events for 2014-02-15:

Here are 10 major events that occurred on February 15th, 2014:

# 2014 Russian anti-war protests
Russia saw widespread protests against the country's involvement in Ukraine, with tens of thousands taking to the streets across the country.

# Edward Snowden granted asylum in Russia
Snowden, the NSA whistleblower, was granted temporary asylum in Russia after fleeing the United States and seeking refuge there.

# North Korea executes 80 people for watching South Korean soap operas
North Korea executed 80 people, including some officials, for watching South Korean soap operas, which were deemed "anti-revolutionary" by the government.

# Pope Francis visits Philippines
Pope Francis visited the Philippines, becoming the first pope to visit the country. He met with victims of Typhoon Haiyan and encouraged the people to continue their efforts in rebuilding.

# US Senate votes on gun control measures
The US Senate voted on a package of gun control measures, including expanded background checks and limits on high-capacity magazines. The measures ultimately failed to pass.

# New York Islanders trade goalie Rick DiPietro to Dallas Stars
In the world of sports, the New York Islanders traded goaltender Rick DiPietro to the Dallas Stars for future considerations.

# Super Bowl XLVIII: Seattle Seahawks defeat Denver Broncos 43-8
The Seattle Seahawks defeated the Denver Broncos in Super Bowl XLVIII by a score of 43-8. The game was played at MetLife Stadium in East Rutherford, New Jersey.

# Malaysian Airline System (MAS) and Qantas announce codeshare agreement
Malaysian Airline System (MAS) and Qantas announced a codeshare agreement that would allow them to share flight codes and offer more services to their passengers.

# 2014 Sochi Winter Paralympics open in Russia
The 2014 Sochi Winter Paralympics opened in Russia, featuring athletes with physical disabilities competing in various winter sports events.

# The Interview film premiere
The comedy film "The Interview" starring Seth Rogen and James Franco premiered at the Sundance Film Festival.
