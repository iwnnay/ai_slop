According to various sources, here are 3-5 major global events that occurred on January 25, 2014:

1. **North Korea executes filmmaker Jang Sung-taek**: North Korean leader Kim Jong-un ordered the execution of his uncle and former regent, Jang Sung-taek, for allegedly plotting to overthrow him. 2. **US Federal Reserve announces tapering of quantitative easing**: The US Federal Open Market Committee announced that it would gradually reduce its bond-buying program (quantitative easing) by $10 billion per month, starting in March 2014. This move was seen as a sign of improving economic conditions and marked a shift towards more normal monetary policy.
3. **Pope Francis canonizes Pope John Paul II**: Pope Francis declared his predecessor, Pope John Paul II, a saint in the Catholic Church. The ceremony took place at the Vatican Basilica, and it marked the first time that a pope was canonized since 1977.
