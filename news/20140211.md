# Events of February 11, 2014
===============


1. **Edward Snowden granted temporary asylum in Russia**: Former NSA contractor Edward Snowden was granted temporary asylum in Russia after fleeing the United States and seeking refuge from extradition to the US for revealing classified information about government surveillance programs.

2. **North Korea executes 15 people accused of plotting against Kim Jong-un**: North Korea executed 15 people accused of plotting against Supreme Leader Kim Jong-un, in a purge that continued a pattern of executions and imprisonments under his rule.

3. **Mitsubishi Motors announces recall of 113,000 vehicles in the US due to faulty airbags**: Mitsubishi Motors announced a massive recall of over 113,000 vehicles in the US due to faulty Takata airbag inflators, which posed a risk of injury or death if deployed in a crash.

4. **Nigerian President Goodluck Jonathan suspends all foreign travel amid kidnapping crisis**: Nigerian President Goodluck Jonathan suspended all foreign travel as his government struggled to contain a wave of kidnappings by Boko Haram militants that had claimed over 100 lives and displaced thousands.

5. **Syrian rebels announce capture of key town near border with Turkey**: Syrian rebel forces announced the capture of the strategic town of Kafr Nabl, which lies near the border with Turkey, in a major blow to government forces.

6. **Brazilian President Dilma Rousseff approves new pension reform bill**: Brazilian President Dilma Rousseff signed into law a comprehensive pension reform bill aimed at addressing the country's growing fiscal crisis and ensuring the long-term viability of its social security system.

7. **New York City Mayor Bill de Blasio announces plan to cut NYPD budget by $95 million**: New York City Mayor Bill de Blasio announced plans to cut the city's police budget by $95 million as part of a broader effort to reduce spending on law enforcement and redirect funds towards education, healthcare, and other social programs.

8. **US Federal Reserve keeps interest rates steady, hints at future rate hike**: The US Federal Reserve kept its benchmark interest rate steady at 0.25% but hinted that it may raise rates in the coming months as the economy shows signs of strengthening.

9. **South Africa's President Jacob Zuma survives no-confidence vote**: South African President Jacob Zuma survived a no-confidence vote in Parliament, with opposition parties failing to muster enough support to oust him from office.

10. **Sochi Winter Paralympics open with ceremony featuring disabled athletes**: The Sochi Winter Paralympics opened with a ceremony featuring disabled athletes and highlighting the importance of inclusivity and accessibility in sports.
