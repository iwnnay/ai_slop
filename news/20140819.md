# Events of August 19, 2014
=====================================================

### News and Politics

1. **Russia's Anti-Gay Law Takes Effect**: Russia's law banning "propaganda" about non-traditional relationships takes effect, sparking international criticism and concern for the treatment of LGBTQ individuals in the country.
2. **Israel-Hamas Ceasefire Holds**: A ceasefire between Israel and Hamas holds after a six-week conflict that saw widespread destruction and thousands of casualties.

### Sports

3. **US Women's National Team Wins World Cup**: The US Women's National Soccer Team defeats Japan 5-2 to win the FIFA Women's World Cup, marking their third title in the tournament.
4. **Serena Williams Wins Toronto Tournament**: Tennis star Serena Williams wins the Rogers Cup women's singles title in Toronto, her sixth title of the year.

### Entertainment

5. **MTV Video Music Awards Red Carpet**: The MTV Video Music Awards take place at The Forum in Inglewood, California, with performances by Taylor Swift, Katy Perry, and Ariana Grande.
6. **Robin Williams Dies**: Legendary actor Robin Williams passes away at age 63, leaving behind a legacy of iconic roles in films like "Aladdin," "Mrs. Doubtfire," and "Good Will Hunting."

### Science and Technology

7. **NASA's LADEE Spacecraft Crashes**: NASA's Lunar Atmosphere and Dust Environment Explorer (LADEE) spacecraft crashes into the moon's surface, marking the end of its mission to study the lunar atmosphere.
8. **Google Acquires Nest for $3.2 Billion**: Google acquires smart home technology company Nest for $3.2 billion, expanding its presence in the Internet of Things market.

### Culture and History

9. **Vatican Confirms Pope Francis' Trip to Philippines**: The Vatican confirms that Pope Francis will visit the Philippines in January 2015, marking his first trip to Asia as pope.
10. **China's Communist Party Celebrates Anniversary**: China's ruling Communist Party celebrates its 95th anniversary, with Xi Jinping and other party leaders attending a ceremony marking the occasion.

These events are significant due to their impact on international politics, sports, entertainment, science, and culture.
