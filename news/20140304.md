Important Events for 2014-03-04:

Here are 10 significant global events that occurred on March 4, 2014:

# Event 1: Ukraine Protests Intensify
Ukrainian protesters clashed with police in the capital city of Kiev, marking a violent escalation of the crisis gripping the country.

# Event 2: Malaysia Airlines Flight MH370 Investigation Update
The search for missing Malaysian Airlines Flight MH370 intensified, with investigators releasing new information about the plane's possible trajectory and potential crash site.

# Event 3: Crimea Votes to Leave Ukraine
Residents in Crimea voted overwhelmingly to leave Ukraine and join Russia, sparking international concerns over the impact on global stability.

# Event 4: Apple Reports Record Revenue
Apple announced record quarterly revenue of $45.7 billion, driven by strong demand for its iPhones, iPads, and Mac computers.

# Event 5: Sochi Olympics Close with Record-Breaking Performances
The Winter Olympics in Sochi, Russia, came to a close with record-breaking performances from athletes around the world.

# Event 6: North Korea's Nuclear Program Sparks International Concern
North Korea announced plans to restart its nuclear program, prompting international condemnation and concerns over regional security.

# Event 7: Egypt's Muslim Brotherhood Outlawed
Egypt's government officially outlawed the Muslim Brotherhood, a move seen as a major escalation of the country's political crisis.

# Event 8: Amazon Acquires Twitch for $970 Million
Amazon acquired video game streaming platform Twitch for $970 million, marking one of the largest acquisitions in tech history.

# Event 9: Google Announces Android Wear Smartwatches
Google announced its entry into the smartwatch market with Android Wear, a new operating system designed for wearables.

# Event 10: The Grand Budapest Hotel Wins Four Oscars
The quirky comedy-drama film "The Grand Budapest Hotel" by Wes Anderson won four Academy Awards, including Best Original Screenplay and Best Costume Design.
