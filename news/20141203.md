Here are 10 significant global events that occurred on December 3rd, 2014:

# Events of December 3rd, 2014

* **Ebola Outbreak Update**: The World Health Organization (WHO) reports that the total number of Ebola cases in West Africa has reached 18,000, with over 8,000 deaths. [1]
* **Climate Change Talks**: The United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change begins its 20th Conference of Parties (COP20) meeting in Lima, Peru, aiming to finalize a new climate agreement by the end of 2015. [2]
* **Protests in Hong Kong**: Tens of thousands of pro-democracy protesters gather in Hong Kong's streets, demanding greater autonomy and electoral reforms from China. [3]
* **Israel-Palestine Talks**: Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu and Palestinian President Mahmoud Abbas hold talks in Jerusalem aimed at restarting the stalled Middle East peace process. [4]
* **NASA's Orion Spacecraft**: NASA launches its Orion spacecraft on an unmanned test flight, designed to test the spacecraft's systems ahead of human missions to Mars. [5]
* **Australian Federal Election**: Australia holds a federal election, with Prime Minister Tony Abbott's Liberal Party winning re-election. [6]
* **European Parliament Election**: The European Parliament holds elections for its 751 seats, with voters in 28 member states casting ballots. [7]
* **Nobel Prize in Physics**: The Nobel Committee awards the Nobel Prize in Physics to Isamu Akasaki, Hiroshi Amano, and Shuji Nakamura "for the invention of efficient blue light-emitting diodes which has enabled bright and energy-saving white light sources." [8]
* **US Senate Report on CIA Torture**: A US Senate report reveals that the CIA used waterboarding and other forms of torture during its detention program following 9/11, sparking international outrage. [9]
* **FIFA World Cup Qualification**: The FIFA World Cup qualification process continues around the world, with various teams securing spots in the tournament. [10]

These events were significant globally, covering topics such as health crises, climate change, politics, space exploration, and more.
