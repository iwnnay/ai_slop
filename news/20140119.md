# Events of January 19th, 2014
==================================================

### International News

1. **Syrian Civil War**: Rebel forces seize control of the strategic town of Kafr Naboudeh in northern Syria.

2. **Ebola Outbreak**: The World Health Organization (WHO) reports a total of 20 cases and 13 deaths from Ebola virus disease in Guinea, Sierra Leone, and Liberia.

### Sports

3. **NBA**: The New York Knicks defeat the Toronto Raptors 92-79 behind Carmelo Anthony's 29 points.

4. **College Football**: Florida State University's Jameis Winston wins the Heisman Trophy for the second consecutive year.

### Entertainment and Culture

5. **Music**: Singer-songwriter Robin Williams dies at age 63 due to an apparent suicide.

6. **Film**: The movie "The Wolf of Wall Street" starring Leonardo DiCaprio is released in theaters.

7. **Literature**: Author Joan Rivers publishes her memoir "I Hate Everyone... Starting with Me".

### Historical Events

8. **Vietnam War**: The USS Pueblo, a US Navy intelligence ship, is seized by North Korean forces on January 19th, 1968, sparking international tensions.

9. **United States Presidential Election**: President Richard Nixon announces his resignation on August 9, 1974, effective at noon the next day, making Gerald Ford the 38th Vice President and subsequent President of the United States.

10. **Cold War**: The Soviet Union launches Luna 9 spacecraft, which becomes the first spacecraft to transmit images from another celestial body, specifically the Moon.

These events represent a mix of significant news, sports, cultural, and historical events that took place on January 19th, 2014.
